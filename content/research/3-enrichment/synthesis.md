## Signal-by-Signal Interpretation

### AGSI -- Germany (DE)

**Raw data:** German storage at 21.52% full (54.04 TWh) as of March 9, down from 26.19% (65.78 TWh) on February 9. Decline of 4.67 percentage points over 28 days. Trajectory inflected around February 20-22: from Feb 9-20, heavy drawdown (withdrawals 1,000-1,750 GWh/day, trend values -0.42 to -0.69). From Feb 22 onward, net injections began -- rates climbed from ~150-370 GWh/day to 550-777 GWh/day in early March, with positive trend values (+0.07 to +0.22). By March 9, injections (621 GWh) exceeded withdrawals (318 GWh).

**Baseline and magnitude:** 21.5% fill in early March is historically very low. 5-year average for this date is closer to 35-40%. Germany is below that floor. Net injection shift is seasonally expected but does not resolve the structural deficit.

**Signal assessment:** STRUCTURAL CONCERN. Germany entered refill season ~15 percentage points below historical norms. The refill math holds: ~60+ bcm needed across EU. Direction: CONFIRMS thesis -- storage vulnerability acute. Germany most storage-dependent major economy.

### AGSI -- Italy (IT)

**Raw data:** Italian storage at 45.79% full (93.12 TWh) on March 9, down from 53.19% (108.16 TWh) on February 9. Decline of 7.4 pp in 28 days. Heavy drawdown through mid-February (700-970 GWh/day). Late February: injection volumes began (90-315 GWh/day), though withdrawals still dominate. Trend values remained negative throughout (-0.01 to -0.47).

**Baseline and magnitude:** 45.8% better than Germany but still below 5-year average. Higher fill reflects Mediterranean LNG regas capacity and Algerian pipeline supply.

**Signal assessment:** MODERATE CONCERN. Italy's relative resilience provides buffer, but slow start to injection season means even better-positioned countries feeling supply pressure. Direction: PARTIALLY MITIGATING.

### AGSI -- Netherlands (NL)

**Raw data:** Dutch storage at 8.97% full (12.95 TWh) on March 9, down from 18.86% (27.21 TWh) on February 9. Catastrophic decline of 9.89 pp in 28 days -- nearly halving fill percentage. Massive withdrawals (200-1,124 GWh/day) against anemic injections (4-240 GWh/day). Every single day shows negative trend. Trend improved from extreme (-0.78) to merely bad (-0.27).

**Baseline and magnitude:** Under 9% is emergency-level. More than 25 points below where it should be -- well beyond the 5pp deviation threshold. The Netherlands is functionally storage-depleted entering refill season.

**Signal assessment:** CRITICAL. Single strongest AGSI signal. Post-Groningen Netherlands depends entirely on imported gas. Refill to any reasonable target from 9% is extraordinarily challenging with Qatari LNG offline. Direction: STRONGLY CONFIRMS thesis.

### AGSI -- Poland (PL)

**Raw data:** Polish storage at 48.51% full (17.62 TWh) on March 9, down from 59.09% (21.46 TWh) on February 9. Decline of 10.58 pp, largest proportional draw. Injections essentially zero most days, withdrawals 45-305 GWh/day. Small injection spikes (40-80 GWh) dwarfed by withdrawals.

**Baseline and magnitude:** 48.5% reasonable in absolute terms, but rate of decline concerning. Poland benefits from Baltic Pipe and Swinoujscie LNG terminal.

**Signal assessment:** MODERATE CONCERN. Near-zero injection through early March suggests supply channels not yet compensating. Direction: PARTIALLY CONFIRMS -- even diversified countries drawing down faster than expected.

### ECB -- EUR/USD Exchange Rate

**Raw data:** EUR/USD from 1.1886 (Feb 9) to 1.1555 (Mar 9 trough), recovering to 1.1641 (Mar 10). Cumulative decline ~2.8% to trough, 2.1% to latest.

**Temporal pattern:** Stable 1.185-1.190 through Feb 13. Gradual drift Feb 14-28 (1.175-1.181). Sharp break March 2 (1.1698), deepening March 3-6 (1.1606-1.1561), bottom March 9 (1.1555). March 2 aligns with QatarEnergy LNG production halt.

**Baseline and magnitude:** Signal framework: 3% cumulative over 2 weeks = structural. Actual: 2.8% over 4 weeks, ~2.2% in March 2-9 week. Approaches but doesn't quite reach structural threshold.

**Signal assessment:** MEANINGFUL BUT NOT YET STRUCTURAL. Euro weakness real, temporally aligned, compounding import costs. Direction: CONFIRMS cost amplification mechanism.
