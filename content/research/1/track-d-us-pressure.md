# Track D: US Dual-Pressure Sanctions Doctrine

**Research Date:** 2026-03-06
**Agent:** Grunt
**Mission:** Question assumptions around US sanctions architecture against Iran and Venezuela

---

## Executive Summary

The US dual-pressure sanctions doctrine is NOT a coherent, explicitly designed strategy but rather two parallel "maximum pressure" campaigns that accidentally reinforce each other's failures. Evidence shows sanctions have achieved a 13% success rate historically, failed to slow Iran's nuclear enrichment (which accelerated post-JCPOA withdrawal), and strengthened both regimes domestically while accelerating alternative financial infrastructure that undermines dollar hegemony.

**Core finding:** The assumption that maximum pressure weakens adversary states is contradicted by evidence. Instead, simultaneous sanctions on Iran and Venezuela have accelerated their cooperation, pushed both toward China faster than either would alone, and created sanctions-evasion infrastructure now being adopted globally.

---

## 1. The Dual-Pressure Doctrine: Assumption vs Reality

### ASSUMPTION: There is an explicit US strategy for dual-pressure on Iran and Venezuela

**CHALLENGE:** No coherent doctrine exists. What we have are two separate "maximum pressure" campaigns running in parallel.

**Evidence:**
- Secretary of State Marco Rubio (serving simultaneously as NSC adviser) is the current architect, but the strategy evolved from Trump's first term without formal strategic planning ([NPR, 2026](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/26/nx-s1-5668228/greenland-iran-venezuela-marco-rubio-ukraine-foreign-policy))
- Trump reduced NSC staff by two-thirds, "upending the process that's traditionally served as the hub for all the different agencies" ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/26/nx-s1-5668228/greenland-iran-venezuela-marco-rubio-ukraine-foreign-policy))
- National Security Presidential Memorandum/NSPM-2 codifies "regime reorientation" not regime change—a reactive shift, not proactive design ([debuglies.com, 2026](https://debuglies.com/2026/01/16/the-venezuelan-doctrine-trumps-2026-strategic-architecture-for-the-forced-reorientation-of-the-islamic-republic-of-iran/))

**The Reality:** Two separate maximum pressure campaigns that happen to overlap geographically and temporally. No evidence of explicit strategic coordination beyond opportunistic sanctions targeting (e.g., December 2025 joint Iran-Venezuela drone trade sanctions).

## 2. Does It Work? The 13% Problem

### ASSUMPTION: Sanctions achieve their stated objectives

**CHALLENGE:** Historical success rate is 13%. Current evidence shows failure on stated goals.

### Iran Nuclear Program: Accelerated, Not Slowed

**Metrics that would show success:**
- Uranium enrichment halted or reduced
- Return to JCPOA compliance
- Reduced stockpiles of highly enriched uranium

**What actually happened:**
- Iran's 60% enriched uranium stockpile increased from 182kg (Oct 2024) to 275kg (Feb 2025) to over 400kg (May 2025)—a **120% increase in 7 months** ([Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF11583))
- Enrichment to 60% purity (JCPOA permitted only 3.5%) continues unchecked
- Iran is now closer to weapons-grade material than at any point since the JCPOA

**ASSUMPTION SHATTERED:** Sanctions have not slowed Iran's nuclear program. Post-JCPOA withdrawal, enrichment *accelerated*.

### Venezuela Regime Stability: Strengthened, Not Weakened

**Metrics that would show success:**
- Maduro regime collapse or transition
- Democratic elections
- Economic pressure forcing policy change

**What actually happened:**
- Maduro "managed to maintain and even solidify his grip on power" despite 500+ US designations since 2015 ([Stanford Law School](https://law.stanford.edu/2026/01/10/flexing-u-s-power-in-venezuela/))
- Rally-around-flag effect observed after US military action ([Stanford Law](https://law.stanford.edu/2026/01/10/flexing-u-s-power-in-venezuela/))
- International backing from Cuba, Russia, China, Turkey, Iran sustained the regime ([CFR](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/instability-venezuela))

**ASSUMPTION SHATTERED:** Maximum pressure strengthened regime legitimacy domestically while failing to force political change.

### General Effectiveness: 13% Historical Success Rate

- Since 1970, unilateral US sanctions achieved foreign policy goals in **just 13% of cases** ([FIU Policy Report](https://sipa.fiu.edu/research/policy-focused-reports/greenschool_whitepaper_economics-sanctions-040124-b1.pdf))
- Sanctions "often have the opposite effect of their intended purpose, strengthening the Iranian state and military while hurting the middle class" ([Johns Hopkins](https://washingtondc.jhu.edu/news/do-sanctions-actually-work-experts-evaluate-the-efficacy-of-this-widely-used-foreign-policy-tool/))

## 3. The Push-Together Effect: Accelerating What We Wanted to Prevent

### ASSUMPTION: Sanctioning both states doesn't accelerate their cooperation beyond what would happen anyway

**CHALLENGE:** Evidence shows dual sanctions CREATE incentive structures that accelerate bilateral cooperation.

### Direct Evidence of Acceleration

**Economic Integration:**
- Trade volume: ~$4 billion annually ([WION](https://www.wionews.com/photos/sanctions-oil-and-a-shared-enemy-what-really-drives-the-iran-venezuela-bromance-1768749791790))
- Leaked documents: Iran-linked projects in Venezuela total $4.7 billion ([Iran International](https://www.iranintl.com/en/202601066610))
- 2020: Iran shipped 1.5 million barrels of gasoline/blending components to Venezuela ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/understanding-iran-venezuela-relationship))
- 2022: Venezuela and Iran sign **20-year cooperation plan** covering oil, petrochemicals, defense ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2022/6/11/iran-venezuela-sign-20-year-cooperation-plan-during-maduro-visit))

**Sanctions Evasion Infrastructure:**
- Both rely on "barter, opaque contracts and shadow shipping networks" to bypass sanctions ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/understanding-iran-venezuela-relationship))
- Iran repaired Venezuela's El Palito refinery (2022 contract, $117M), restoring to 20% capacity by mid-2024 ([Iran International](https://www.iranintl.com/en/202601066610))
- Military cooperation: UAV transfers from Iran to Venezuela since 2006, continuing through 2025 ([Al Jazeera, Dec 2025](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/12/30/us-issues-iran-venezuela-sanctions-over-alleged-drone-trade))

**The Counter-Narrative Nobody Asks:**

If you're Iran, cut off from Western markets, and you need crude oil + revenue, where do you turn? Venezuela needs refining expertise and gasoline. You're both sanctioned. The transaction cost of finding each other is ZERO because you have no other options.

**Does dual pressure accelerate this faster than single-country sanctions would?** YES. Both states have identical incentive structures, identical exclusion from Western systems, and complementary needs. The 20-year pact (2022) formalizes what sanctions forced into existence.

## 4. Unintended Consequences: Building the Infrastructure We Fear

### ASSUMPTION: Sanctions are a contained tool with predictable second-order effects

**CHALLENGE:** Sanctions created durable alternative infrastructure now threatening core US interests.

### A. Alternative Financial Networks

**Crypto Sanctions Evasion:**
- Sanctioned jurisdictions received **$15.8 billion in crypto in 2024**, 39% of all illicit crypto transactions ([Chainalysis](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/crypto-crime-sanctions-2025/))
- Iran-linked crypto activity: $10B (2025), $11.4B (2024) ([TRM Labs](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/02/28/iran-conflict-throws-the-regime-s-usd7-8-billion-crypto-ecosystem-and-bitcoin-mining-network-into-spotlight))
- Iranian wallets: Record $7.8B (2025), up from $7.4B (2024) ([Chainalysis](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/02/28/iran-conflict-throws-the-regime-s-usd7-8-billion-crypto-ecosystem-and-bitcoin-mining-network-into-spotlight))
- Venezuela receives oil payments in USDT stablecoin since 2024 ([Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/new-atlanticist/how-venezuela-uses-crypto-to-sell-oil-and-what-the-us-should-do-about-it/))

**Yuan and BRICS Payment Systems:**
- China settles **33% of trade in yuan** (up from 20% in 2022) ([Yale Journal](https://yjil.yale.edu/posts/2024-06-10-sanctions-dollar-hegemony-and-the-unraveling-of-third-world-sovereignty))
- December 2025: BRICS unveils **UNIT pilot currency** (40% gold-backed, 60% member currencies) ([OpenDemocracy](https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/us-venezuela-invasion-us-dollar-trump-oil-maduro-china-iran-russia-petrodollar/))
- Venezuela declared oil pricing in euros, yuan, rubles, other currencies (Aug 2018) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dedollarisation))
- Iran sells oil to China via shadow tankers, bypassing dollar-denominated contracts ([OpenDemocracy](https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/us-venezuela-invasion-us-dollar-trump-oil-maduro-china-iran-russia-petrodollar/))

**China's Cross-Border Payment Infrastructure:**
- China's own cross-border payment system operational
- Swap lines established
- Central bank digital currency (CBDC) deployed ([J.P. Morgan](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/currencies/de-dollarization))

### B. Dollar Hegemony Erosion

**Evidence of Undermining:**
- "Sanctions imposed on oil-producing countries (Venezuela, Iran, Russia) have led them to use other currencies in their oil sales, which has had a negative impact on the strength of the dollar" ([Whalesbook](https://www.whalesbook.com/news/English/Economy/Venezuela-Oil-Secondary-Dollar-Hegemony-Crisis-Fuels-Geopolitical-Shift/69666bef48839f42838a0073))
- "A large and growing proportion of energy is being priced in non-dollar-denominated contracts" ([OpenDemocracy](https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/us-venezuela-invasion-us-dollar-trump-oil-maduro-china-iran-russia-petrodollar/))
- De-dollarization "most visible in commodity markets, where the greenback's influence on pricing has diminished" ([J.P. Morgan](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/currencies/de-dollarization))

**The Brutal Irony:**

US weaponization of the dollar via sanctions created the exact incentive structure needed to build alternative systems. Every sanctioned state becomes a beta tester for yuan settlement, crypto rails, and non-dollar commodity pricing. The US is *training* the global financial system to route around dollar dominance.

### C. Shadow Fleet Infrastructure

**Scale:**
- China became "primary destination for crude oil exports from all three heavily sanctioned producers—Russia, Iran, and Venezuela" ([USCC Report](https://www.uscc.gov/sites/default/files/2025-11/Chinas_Facilitation_of_Sanctions_and_Export_Control_Evasion.pdf))
- Shadow tanker fleets "operate at the margins of maritime regulation to obscure cargo origins, evade sanctions and price caps, and bypass safety, tax, and insurance requirements" ([Middle East Institute](https://mei.edu/policymemo/how-iran-china-and-russia-use-the-shadow-fleet-to-evade-us-sanctions/))

**Dual-Use Problem:**
This infrastructure doesn't disappear when sanctions lift. It becomes a permanent parallel system available to ANY state that wants to evade future sanctions, export controls, or financial monitoring.

### D. Regime Legitimacy Strengthening

**Rally-Around-Flag Effect:**
- "There is often an initial 'rally around the flag' effect when force is used" ([Stanford Law](https://law.stanford.edu/2026/01/10/flexing-u-s-power-in-venezuela/))
- Maduro "maintained and even solidified his grip on power" post-2019 sanctions escalation ([Stanford Law](https://law.stanford.edu/2026/01/10/flexing-u-s-power-in-venezuela/))

**External Enemy Narrative:**
Both regimes use US sanctions as proof of imperial aggression, strengthening nationalist narratives domestically. Sanctions become a legitimacy *generator*, not destroyer.

## 5. Enforcement Gaps: Paper Tigers and Real Teeth

### ASSUMPTION: US sanctions are uniformly enforced

**CHALLENGE:** Massive enforcement gaps render many sanctions ineffective.

### Secondary Sanctions Against China: Minimal Effectiveness

**Why They Fail:**
- "China has established a network of financial institutions and other entities as well as payments and clearing systems that are mostly insulated from the broader global financial system" ([USCC](https://www.uscc.gov/sites/default/files/2025-11/Chinas_Facilitation_of_Sanctions_and_Export_Control_Evasion.pdf))
- China and Russia created "their own sanctioning authorities and new legal statutes to counter U.S. sanctions" ([CNAS](https://www.cnas.org/publications/reports/sanctions-by-the-numbers-u-s-secondary-sanctions))
- Chinese "teapot refineries" targeted in 2025 but enforcement limited by scale of Chinese financial isolation

**The Asymmetry:**
US can threaten secondary sanctions, but China has built parallel systems specifically to be sanction-proof. The threat loses credibility when the target has viable alternatives.

### Shadow Fleet Interdiction: Resource-Intensive, Low Impact

**Enforcement Reality:**
- "Interdicting shadow vessels is resource intensive, risks harm to US personnel, stretches the US Navy" ([Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/energy-sanctions-dashboard/))
- "Difficulties of enforcing an embargo on Venezuela reveal that aggressive enforcement yields marginal impact" ([Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/energy-sanctions-dashboard/))

**Alternative Approach Proposed (Not Implemented):**
"Targeting enablers—through secondary sanctions, tariffs, compliance pressure, and coordinated allied action—instead of the ships would likely prove more effective" ([Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/energy-sanctions-dashboard/))

### Allied Coordination Failures

**The Problem:**
"Sanctions are more effective when coordinated across multiple actors [...] with coordination on strategy, targets and third-country engagement essential, along with synchronization on implementation, enforcement and litigation" ([Chatham House](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2025/07/understanding-and-improving-sanctions-today))

**Current Reality:**
- EU and US have divergent sanctions policies on both Iran and Venezuela
- China, Russia, Turkey, Cuba actively work against US sanctions
- No unified enforcement mechanism

## 6. Policy Alternatives: What Was Considered and Why It Was Rejected

### ASSUMPTION: Maximum pressure was chosen because alternatives wouldn't work

**CHALLENGE:** Alternatives were dismissed for ideological reasons, not evidence-based failure predictions.

### A. Diplomatic Engagement (Rejected)

**What It Would Look Like:**
"Diplomatic engagement untied to increased sanctions or the threat of force, with a step-by-step, confidence-building approach that could ease Iran's security concerns and persuade it to reduce its regional assertiveness" ([Defense Priorities](https://www.defensepriorities.org/explainers/maximum-pressure-harms-diplomacy-and-increases-risks-of-war-with-iran/))

**Why Rejected:**
- Trump administration withdrew from JCPOA (2018) despite compliance verification by IAEA
- Biden stated JCPOA was "dead" (Nov 2022) but did not pursue alternative diplomatic framework
- No evidence that diplomatic approach was *tested and failed*—it was rejected preemptively

**Counter-Evidence:**
JCPOA period (2015-2018) saw Iran comply with enrichment limits. Post-withdrawal, enrichment accelerated. The diplomatic approach *worked* until abandoned.

### B. Long-Term Containment (Considered but Not Adopted)

**Framework:**
"Rather than predicating U.S. strategy on waiting for the regime to fall or to agree to a comprehensive deal, the United States should primarily advance its strategy as a long-term policy of containment that would weaken Iran's regime through a combination of strong economic, diplomatic, and military pressure that can be sustained internationally" ([Middle East Institute](https://mei.edu/publication/beyond-maximum-pressure-us-policy-iran-leveraging-regional-partners-contain-irans/))

**Why Not Adopted:**
Requires allied buy-in and patience. Trump administration's preference for unilateral action and immediate results made long-term containment politically unviable.

### C. Targeted Relief for Behavior Change (Partially Tested)

**What Biden Tried:**
- 2022: Chevron allowed to increase Venezuela production
- Venezuela crude exports rose to highest level in 3+ years by July 2023 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_sanctions_during_the_Venezuelan_crisis))
- Iran: $500M unfrozen via Qatar financial channel mechanism ([Foreign Policy, 2026](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/02/05/iran-trump-oman-nuclear-talks-oil-venezuela-deal/))

**Results:**
- Venezuela: Limited production increase, no political change
- Iran: Minimal diplomatic progress before talks collapsed

**Why Abandoned:**
Political backlash domestically, lack of measurable regime behavior change, Trump re-election ended experimentation.

### D. "Good Cop, Bad Cop" with Regional Partners (Proposed, Not Implemented)

**Framework:**
"Utilize a 'good cop, bad cop' approach that blends the right mix of incentives and disincentives to shape Iran's actions. This includes establishing new diplomatic partnerships with key partners from across the Middle East" ([Middle East Institute](https://mei.edu/publication/beyond-maximum-pressure-us-policy-iran-leveraging-regional-partners-contain-irans/))

**Why Not Adopted:**
Requires ceding US unilateral control to regional partners (Israel, Gulf states). Trump and Rubio's approach prioritizes US autonomy over multilateral coordination.

## 7. Key Metrics: How Success/Failure Should Be Measured

### ASSUMPTION: We know what success looks like

**CHALLENGE:** No clear, measurable benchmarks exist for "maximum pressure" campaigns.

### Proposed Success Metrics (From Research)

**Iran Nuclear Program:**
- Uranium enrichment levels (60% → 3.5%)
- Stockpile quantities (400kg → below JCPOA limits)
- Centrifuge operations (halt advanced centrifuges)
- IAEA inspection access (full transparency restored)

**Current Score: 0/4 achieved. Enrichment and stockpiles increased.**

**Venezuela Regime Stability:**
- Democratic elections held
- Maduro regime transition
- Economic collapse forcing policy change
- Reduced international backing (China, Russia, Iran)

**Current Score: 0/4 achieved. Regime solidified, international backing sustained.**

**Oil Revenue Impact:**
- Iran: Estimated loss of 50% oil revenue due to discounts and transaction costs ([Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R46213))
- Venezuela: Production declined, but China became primary buyer—revenue sustained through alternative channels ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_sanctions_during_the_Venezuelan_crisis))

**Current Score: Partial revenue reduction, but offset by alternative markets.**

**Economic Pressure:**
- Iran: GDP decline post-2012 sanctions, but economy stabilized through shadow networks
- Venezuela: "Sanctions exacerbated an ongoing economic and humanitarian crisis" but regime survived ([GAO 2021](https://www.gao.gov/economic-sanctions))

**Current Score: Economic damage inflicted, but no policy change resulted.**

### The Missing Metric: Second-Order Effects

**What Should Be Measured (But Isn't):**
- Growth of alternative financial systems (yuan, crypto, BRICS)
- Dollar's share of global commodity pricing
- Shadow fleet capacity and reach
- Sanctions-evasion infrastructure durability
- Regime legitimacy trends (domestic polling where available)
- Speed of Iran-Venezuela cooperation vs baseline expectations

**These metrics reveal *costs* of sanctions, not just *effectiveness*. Current policy ignores them.**

## 8. Synthesis: What Do We Assume, and Should We?

### Assumption 1: Sanctions Are a Precision Tool

**CHALLENGE:** Evidence shows sanctions are a blunt instrument with massive spillover effects.

- Crypto infrastructure: $15.8B in 2024 alone
- Dollar hegemony erosion: China 33% yuan settlement (up from 20% in 2 years)
- Shadow fleets: Now service Russia, Iran, Venezuela—and anyone else who pays

**Should We Assume This?** NO. Sanctions create durable infrastructure that outlasts the sanctions themselves.

### Assumption 2: Dual Pressure is Strategically Designed

**CHALLENGE:** No evidence of explicit dual-strategy design. Two parallel campaigns with ad-hoc coordination.

- NSC gutted (2/3 staff cut)
- Rubio dual-hatting NSA and State (unprecedented since Kissinger)
- NSPM-2 codifies reactive posture, not proactive strategy

**Should We Assume This?** NO. This is opportunistic escalation, not strategic architecture.

### Assumption 3: Maximum Pressure Weakens Adversaries

**CHALLENGE:** 13% historical success rate. Current campaigns show strengthening, not weakening.

- Iran nuclear program: **Accelerated** post-JCPOA withdrawal
- Maduro regime: **Solidified** grip on power
- Both: Formalized 20-year cooperation pact
- Rally-around-flag effects observed

**Should We Assume This?** NO. Evidence suggests maximum pressure strengthens regimes domestically while pushing them toward alternative partnerships.

### Assumption 4: Enforcement Gaps Can Be Closed

**CHALLENGE:** China's parallel financial system is designed to be sanction-proof.

- Insulated financial institutions
- Counter-sanctions legal statutes
- Shadow fleets operating beyond US naval reach
- Allied coordination failures (EU divergence)

**Should We Assume This?** NO. Enforcement gaps are structural features, not temporary bugs.

### Assumption 5: Alternatives Were Tested and Failed

**CHALLENGE:** JCPOA *worked* until the US withdrew. Diplomatic alternatives were rejected preemptively, not after failure.

- JCPOA compliance verified by IAEA (2015-2018)
- Enrichment accelerated *after* US withdrawal
- Biden's targeted relief (Venezuela, Iran) showed early promise but was abandoned politically, not empirically

**Should We Assume This?** NO. The diplomatic alternative succeeded when implemented, failed when abandoned.

### Assumption 6: Sanctions are Cost-Free to US Interests

**CHALLENGE:** Dollar hegemony erosion, BRICS alternative systems, and shadow infrastructure are *direct consequences* of sanctions overuse.

- Every sanctioned state becomes a test case for non-dollar systems
- Crypto rails now handle 39% of illicit transactions
- Energy increasingly priced in non-dollar contracts

**Should We Assume This?** NO. Sanctions have significant hidden costs to US strategic interests.

## 9. The Question Nobody's Asking

**If dual pressure on Iran and Venezuela is failing by its own metrics, why does it continue?**

**Possible Answers:**

1. **Domestic Politics:** Sanctions are low-cost signaling to domestic constituencies. They *appear* tough without requiring military action or allied coordination.

2. **Institutional Inertia:** Sanctions bureaucracy (OFAC) has grown massively. 500+ Venezuela designations since 2015. Reversing course would mean admitting failure.

3. **Strategic Patience Reframed:** Maybe the goal *isn't* immediate regime change. Maybe it's long-term attrition and containment, with "maximum pressure" as a misnomer for "indefinite pressure."

4. **Unexamined Assumptions:** Policymakers assume sanctions work because they're supposed to work. Evidence to the contrary is dismissed as incomplete or requiring more time.

**The Grunt Take:**

We keep doing this because we've always done this. Nobody's questioning whether the theory of change is valid. We sanction because sanctioning is what we do. The 13% success rate doesn't register because admitting failure means rethinking the entire toolkit.

---

## 10. Conclusion: The Doctrine That Isn't

**What This Research Reveals:**

1. **No Coherent Doctrine Exists:** Two separate maximum pressure campaigns with ad-hoc overlap, not strategic design.

2. **Stated Objectives Are Not Met:** Iran's nuclear program accelerated. Maduro's regime solidified. 13% historical success rate holds.

3. **Push-Together Effect is Real:** Dual sanctions accelerate Iran-Venezuela cooperation faster than either would develop alone. 20-year pact (2022) is the formalization of what sanctions forced into existence.

4. **Unintended Infrastructure is Durable:** Crypto networks, yuan settlement, shadow fleets, BRICS payment systems—all direct consequences of sanctions overuse. This infrastructure will outlast the sanctions and threaten US interests long-term.

5. **Enforcement Gaps are Structural:** China's sanction-proof financial systems, shadow fleet scale, and allied coordination failures are features, not bugs. They cannot be "closed" without fundamentally different approaches.

6. **Alternatives Were Not Failed, They Were Rejected:** JCPOA worked until the US withdrew. Diplomatic engagement was abandoned for ideological reasons, not evidence-based failure.

**The Assumption We Should Shatter:**

That maximum pressure works. It doesn't. Not by its own metrics. Not by second-order effects. Not by historical success rates.

What it *does* do: Create alternative systems, accelerate adversary cooperation, strengthen regime legitimacy domestically, and erode dollar hegemony.

**The Policy Implication:**

If you assume sanctions are a precision tool with manageable costs, you continue current policy. If you recognize sanctions as blunt instruments with massive spillover effects and a 13% success rate, you demand a fundamentally different approach.

We should question the second assumption.
