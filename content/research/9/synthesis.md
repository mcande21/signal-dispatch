## Signal-by-Signal Interpretation

**Web Research (analytical backbone).** H.R. 22 passed House 220-208, cleared Senate procedural vote 51-48 on March 17, 2026, but far short of 60 votes for cloture. Federal bill almost certainly dead. Real action is state-level: FL (signed, 2027), NH (HB 1569, trial underway), WY (signed, July 2025), SD/UT (pending before 2026). CDCE survey: 9.1% (21.3M) lack ready access. Partisan breakdown: 13% Independents, 10% Democrats, 7% Republicans. 2024 naturalized citizen vote: 51% Harris / 47% Trump (vs 59-38 Biden). Kansas 800:1 ratio (31,000 citizens blocked per ~39 noncitizens).

**Census demographics (quantitative backbone).** 440 CDs analyzed. 262 CDs have >10% naturalized citizen share of CVAP. 149 CDs exceed 20%. Top CDs overwhelmingly safe Democratic: NY-6 (88.9%), FL-26 (88.5%), CA-17 (83.7%). Competitive race overlap: GA-7 (44.7% naturalized, 208K citizens), CA-45 (52.4%), NC-12 (20.0%, 103K citizens), AZ-1 (15.6%, 96K citizens). The razor-thin races are barely affected: WI-3 has only 3.1% naturalized share (18K people), IA-1 has 5.7% (35K). Senate: AZ 13.6% (893K), GA 11.6% (1.09M), NC 9.4% (854K).

**CPS registration barriers.** Of 8,229 records (26.9M weighted), documentation barrier (PES3=3) accounts for only 2.4% (646,508 weighted). Dominant barriers: ineligibility (44.1%), disinterest (17.1%), not knowing how (12.0%). By citizenship: native-born 2.1%, naturalized 2.9%, non-citizen 4.2%. Racial breakdown: predominantly white (462,947) with significant Hispanic (224,156). National turnout gap: native-born 77.6% vs naturalized 67.9% (9.7pp). Competitive states: WI 16.1pp gap, IA 19.5pp, GA 14.7pp, MI 14.2pp.

**Historical elections.** Arizona (Prop 200, 2004): no clean before/after signal — redistricting, national environment, and candidate quality dominate. Kansas: Sharice Davids won KS-3 with 53.6% in 2018 DURING the Kobach regime — backlash mobilization may offset suppression. Georgia database-check model caught 1,634 noncitizen attempts without blocking eligible citizens.

**Legislative and markets.** Kalshi House popular vote: market-implied median ~D+7-8 (more bearish on Rs than Silver Bulletin D+5.3). Senate: GA-D 79.5%, MI-D 79%, NH-D 84.5%. No SAVE Act-specific markets. NH at 84.5% may not adequately price HB 1569 implementation risk.

## The Partisan Asymmetry Question

**Where conventional narrative holds:**
- GA-7: 208,635 naturalized citizens (44.7% CVAP). 2-3% facing friction = 4,000-6,000 votes.
- NC-12: 102,962 naturalized (20.0%), 44,810 Hispanic, 17,744 Black.
- AZ-1: 95,824 naturalized (15.6%), 32,827 Hispanic.
- GA Senate: 1,091,821 naturalized citizens statewide (11.6%). 1-2pp additional suppression ≈ 11,000-22,000 voters.

**Where conventional narrative breaks:**
- WI-3 (margin <12K) has only 3.1% naturalized share. IA-1 (margin <800) has 5.7%. The tightest races are the least affected by naturalization-axis suppression.
- 8 of 10 lowest-passport-rate CDs are Republican-held (Southern).
- 62% Evangelical hub adults, 58% working-class, 53% rural lack passports — core Republican constituencies.
- 2024 naturalized citizen realignment: no longer reliably Democratic.
- Independents (13% lack access) are the biggest losers. In 2026, breaking anti-Trump.

**Net quantification (rough):** ~2,220 Democratic net voter loss per competitive CD, assuming CDCE rates, 50% effective suppression, typical party splits. Meaningful in razor-thin races but not determinative. Partially offset by mobilization backlash and adaptation.

## Cross-Signal Convergence

All tracks agree:
1. Noncitizen voting is vanishingly rare (CPS: zero; Kansas: 39 in 19 years; Heritage: 77-99 nationally over 25 years)
2. 2026 environment hostile to Republicans independent of SAVE Act (39% approval, D+5.3)
3. Documentation gaps are real and disproportionately affect low-income, minority, young, rural
4. Most competitive 2026 races are NOT in high-naturalized-citizen districts

Divergence:
1. Academic evidence disagrees (GAO: 1.9-3.2pp decline; NBER: no significant effect)
2. Kansas data points both ways (31K blocked AND Davids won during Kobach)
3. CPS barrier data may undercount documentation effect under prospective DPOC regime

## Net Directional Assessment

**Slight net benefit to Republicans, narrow and contingent.** DPOC creates friction disproportionately on Democratic-leaning and Independent demographics. But the effect is small relative to macro environment, partially offset by mobilization backlash and adaptation, and partially offset by 2024 naturalized citizen realignment.

State-level: only New Hampshire has both a competitive federal race AND active DPOC law for 2026. Everything else is safe states or post-2026.

## The State-Level Story

Federal SAVE Act is legislative theater. For 2026:
1. **New Hampshire (HB 1569)** — only state with competitive race + active DPOC. Trial ruling expected before Sept 2026 primary.
2. **Wyoming** — effective July 2025 but no competitive races.
3. **South Dakota, Utah** — pending, no competitive races.
4. **Florida** — signed but 2027 effective. Massive 2028 impact (4.4M naturalized citizens).
