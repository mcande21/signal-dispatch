## A. THE LAW ITSELF

**H.R. 22 (SAVE Act) requires:**
- Documentary proof of U.S. citizenship (DPOC) at voter registration for all federal elections
- Accepted documents: U.S. passport alone, OR certified birth certificate + government-issued photo ID, REAL ID-enhanced driver's licenses (5 states only), military ID with U.S. birth notation, naturalization certificates, consular birth reports
- State use of DHS SAVE database to verify citizenship and remove ineligible registrants
- Mandatory voter roll purges based on SAVE database cross-referencing
- Criminal penalties: up to 5 years imprisonment for election officials who register without proper documentation, even if the voter is a citizen

**Legislative status:**
- Passed House 218-213 on February 11, 2026
- Senate procedural vote March 17, 2026: cleared 51-48. Murkowski (R-AK) only Republican voting against.
- Final passage requires 60 votes (cloture). Republicans well short. David Becker (CEIR): "extremely unlikely, if not impossible"
- Trump has requested amendments adding: elimination of mail-in voting, restrictions on gender-affirming surgery, transgender athletic provisions

**Treatment of existing registrations:**
NOT grandfathered. Re-verification triggered by any "registration transaction" — moving, name change, party switch. ~27M address changes annually (USPS). Creates rolling re-verification burden affecting millions each cycle.

**Implementation timeline:**
Takes effect immediately upon enactment. EAC must issue guidance within 10 days. No phase-in. Election administrators describe compliance as requiring "tens, perhaps hundreds of millions of dollars" with no federal funding.

## B. WHO LACKS DOCUMENTATION

**Brennan Center / CDCE 2023 survey (n=2,386):**
- 9.1% of voting-age citizens (21.3M) lack ready access to citizenship documents
- 3.8M lack documents entirely
- By race: 11% citizens of color vs 8% white citizens
- By party: Independents 13%, Democrats 10%, Republicans 7%. (Independents 4% have NO documents, Democrats 2%, Republicans 1%)
- By age: 18-24 year olds most affected (24.34%)

**Passport possession:**
- National: ~45-50% hold valid passport
- By income: 21% under $50K vs 64% over $100K
- By race: Black 34%, White 42%, Hispanic 55%
- By geography: South 33%, Midwest 35%, Northeast 40%, West 45%
- By affiliation: 62% of Evangelical hub adults lack passports; 58% working-class; 53% rural

**Critical cross-cutting finding (National Redistricting Foundation):**
- All 10 CDs with LOWEST passport rates are in the South. 8 of 10 are Republican-held.
- 9 of 10 CDs with HIGHEST passport rates are held by Democrats (6 in CA, plus MA, NY, VA)
- Passport application fees: $165+

**Birth certificate access:**
- Rural residents 2x more likely to lack birth certificate or passport
- ~4.5M rural Americans lack required documents
- Elderly and Black Americans in the South disproportionately affected (home births, pre-hospital era)
- Replacement costs $5-23 but "bureaucratic doom loop" for name changes, pre-digital records, cross-state moves

## C. STATE PRECEDENT

**Arizona Prop 200 (2004):**
- 30,000+ registration applications rejected. 17% of rejected applicants were Latino.
- SCOTUS ruled 7-2 in 2013 (Arizona v. Inter Tribal Council): proof-of-citizenship preempted by NVRA for federal forms
- Arizona maintains bifurcated system: DPOC for state/local, not required for federal-only registration

**Kansas Kobach Law (2013-2018):**
- 31,000+ applicants blocked. 75% were motor-voter applicants.
- Court finding: over 19 years, at most 39 noncitizens managed to register — "incredibly slight evidence"
- **Ratio: ~800 eligible citizens blocked per noncitizen caught**
- Struck down by federal judge (NVRA + 14th Amendment). 10th Circuit affirmed. SCOTUS declined cert.
- Judge ordered Kobach to take continuing legal education courses.

**Georgia database-check model:**
- Uses DHS SAVE database cross-reference (not documentary proof at point of registration)
- Found 1,634 noncitizen registration attempts. All placed in pending status. None voted.
- 100% REAL ID compliant — everyone with state license already proved citizenship
- Caught attempts WITHOUT blocking eligible citizens — fundamentally different approach

**New Hampshire HB 1569 (signed 2024):**
- Requires passport, birth certificate, naturalization papers at registration
- Eliminated affidavit option
- Federal trial began Feb 10, 2026. Ruling expected before Sept 2026 primary.
- Legislature introduced separate bill to help impoverished voters comply — tacit admission of burden

**Wyoming HB 156 (signed Feb 2025, effective July 1, 2025)**

**States with pending bills:** Florida (signed, effective 2027), South Dakota and Utah (before 2026 midterms), Louisiana (enacted), 15 total states with bills in 2025-2026 sessions

## D. ACADEMIC STUDIES

**GAO-14-634 (2014):**
- Kansas/Tennessee turnout declined 1.9-3.2pp more than comparison states after voter ID
- Larger impact on: 18-23 year olds, registrants <1 year, African Americans
- Limitation: 2 states, 1 election

**NBER Working Paper 25522 (Cantoni & Pons, 2019):**
- 1.6B observations, 2008-2018 panel
- No statistically significant negative effect on registration or turnout for any demographic group
- Also found: no effect on fraud, actual or perceived

**Why they disagree:** GAO measured immediate impact (before adaptation). NBER captured longer-run equilibrium where mobilization and adaptation may dampen initial effects. CRITICALLY: neither studied proof-of-citizenship specifically — both studied photo voter ID, which is LESS burdensome than DPOC.

**Lipkovitz (2025):** Late-adopting states (post-2008) see 2.7pp turnout decrease. Early-adopting states show no significant effect — suggesting adaptation over time.

## F. PARTISAN MAPPING — THE HARD QUESTION

**Conventional narrative:** SAVE Act hurts Democrats by suppressing minority/immigrant voters.

**What the data shows — far more ambiguous:**

1. **Naturalized citizens shifted dramatically in 2024.** 51% Harris / 47% Trump (vs 59-38 Biden 2020). White naturalized: 55% Trump. Hispanic naturalized: 51% Trump. No longer solidly Democratic.

2. **Republicans MORE likely to lack documentation in many measures.** CDCE: 7% Republicans vs 10% Democrats vs 13% Independents lack ready access. BUT 8 of 10 lowest-passport-rate CDs are Republican-held (Southern).

3. **Republican-leaning demographics heavily affected.** 62% Evangelical hub adults lack passports. 58% working-class. 53% rural. Core Republican constituencies.

4. **Elderly cross-cut:** Skew Republican, among most likely to lack birth certificates (home births, pre-hospital era, lost documents).

5. **Youth cross-cut:** 18-24 most affected, lean Democratic but lowest turnout. Marginal deterred voter is young — but many wouldn't have voted anyway.

6. **Independents are biggest losers.** 13% lack ready access. Independents determine elections. In 2026, breaking hard anti-Trump.

**Net assessment:** Effect is NOT clearly directional. Both parties lose through different mechanisms. Democrats: minority and naturalized citizen suppression. Republicans: elderly, rural, working-class, evangelical suppression. Net depends on geography, competitive races, and Independent lean.

In 2026 specifically, with Independents breaking anti-Trump, SAVE Act likely provides slight net benefit to Republicans — but narrow and contingent, not structural.

## G. PROPONENT ARGUMENTS

**Evidence for noncitizen voting:**
- Heritage Foundation: 77-99 cases nationally, 1999-2025
- CPS self-report: flagged as measurement error by CPS researchers
- Iowa audit: initial claim 2,176 → investigated → 35 actual voters (0.01%)
- Michigan: 16 instances in 2024 (0.00028% of votes)
- Texas: of 2,724 SAVE-flagged, many confirmed citizens
- Georgia: 1,634 attempts caught by database check. None voted.
- CEIR: Initial claims "almost certain to be a misleading overestimation." Never exceeds "a few tenths of one percent."

**Public opinion:** Harvard CAPS/Harris: 71% overall support (91% R, 69% I, 50% D). But support depends heavily on question framing.

## H. LEGAL LANDSCAPE

**Constitutional vulnerabilities:**
- 24th Amendment (poll tax): weak — construed narrowly
- Equal Protection (14th Amendment): STRONGEST avenue
- Voting Rights Act: disparate impact on minorities
- Due Process: purges without adequate notice
- NVRA preemption: Kansas precedent directly addresses

**Active litigation:**
- Brennan Center, ACLU, ACLU-DC, Legal Defense Fund, LatinoJustice, AAJC sued to block
- NH HB 1569 trial underway, ruling expected before Sept 2026 primary
- Injunction likelihood if passed: HIGH (Kansas + Arizona SCOTUS precedents)

## I. SECOND-ORDER EFFECTS

- **Kansas ratio:** 31,000 citizens blocked per ~39 noncitizens = 800:1
- **Administrative cost:** $510M/cycle (NACo), 11.3x HAVA funding. Zero federal funding.
- **Election official chilling effect:** 5-year imprisonment provision
- **Mobilization backlash:** NBER found no clear counter-mobilization from voter ID. But SAVE Act specifically generating significant organizational activity.
- **Mail/online registration:** 7M mail + 11M online registrations in 2022 would require complete overhaul

## KEY ANALYTICAL FINDINGS

1. SAVE Act unlikely to pass Senate. But state-level equivalents ARE becoming law.
2. Partisan impact genuinely ambiguous — conventional narrative is half the story.
3. 2026 environment historically hostile to incumbent party. SAVE Act is marginal on top of macro forces.
4. Real story is state-level implementation. SD/UT before 2026 midterms.
5. Noncitizen voting evidence is thin. Kansas ratio (800:1) is the most damning counterpoint.
