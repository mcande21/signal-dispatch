## The Law

The Safeguard American Voter Eligibility Act (H.R. 22) requires documentary proof of U.S. citizenship (DPOC) at voter registration for all federal elections. Accepted documents: U.S. passport, certified birth certificate + photo ID, REAL ID driver's license (5 states), military ID with U.S. birth notation, naturalization certificate, consular birth report.

Additional provisions: mandatory voter roll purges via DHS SAVE database cross-referencing. Criminal penalties up to 5 years for election officials who register without proper documentation. Existing registrations NOT grandfathered — re-verification triggered by any registration transaction (address change, name change, party switch). USPS reports ~27 million address changes annually.

Legislative status: Passed House 218-213 (Feb 11, 2026). Senate procedural vote cleared 51-48 (March 17, 2026). Final passage requires 60 votes. Zero Democratic cosponsors. 110 Republican cosponsors.

State-level equivalents already enacted or pending: New Hampshire (HB 1569, signed 2024, trial underway), Wyoming (signed Feb 2025), Florida (signed, effective 2027), South Dakota and Utah (pending before 2026 midterms), Louisiana (enacted). 15 states total with active bills.

## Who Lacks Documentation

CDCE/Brennan Center 2023 survey (n=2,386 U.S. citizens 18+):
- 9.1% of voting-age citizens (21.3 million) lack ready access to citizenship documents
- 3.8 million lack documents entirely
- By race: 11% of citizens of color vs 8% of white citizens
- By party: 13% of Independents, 10% of Democrats, 7% of Republicans lack ready access
- By age: 18-24 year olds most affected (24.34%)
- Independents: 4% have NO documents at all; Democrats 2%; Republicans 1%

Passport possession:
- National: ~45-50% hold valid passport
- By income: 21% under $50K vs 64% over $100K
- 8 of 10 congressional districts with LOWEST passport rates are Republican-held (Southern)
- 9 of 10 districts with HIGHEST passport rates are Democratic-held
- 62% of Evangelical hub adults lack valid passports
- 58% of working-class adults lack passports
- 53% of rural middle Americans lack passports
- Passport application fees: $165+

Birth certificate access:
- Rural residents 2x more likely to lack birth certificate or passport
- ~4.5 million rural Americans lack required documents
- Elderly and Black Americans in the South disproportionately affected

## Historical Precedent

Kansas Kobach Law (2013-2018):
- Required birth certificate, naturalization papers, or passport to register
- 31,000+ eligible citizens blocked from registering
- 75% were motor-voter applicants
- Court finding: over 19 years, at most 39 noncitizens registered
- Ratio: ~800 eligible citizens blocked per noncitizen caught
- 10th Circuit struck down; SCOTUS declined cert
- During the Kobach regime, Sharice Davids (D) won KS-3 with 53.6% in 2018 — first Democrat to win a Kansas House seat in a decade

Arizona Prop 200 (2004):
- 30,000+ registration applications rejected. 17% of rejected applicants were Latino.
- SCOTUS ruled 7-2: preempted by NVRA for federal voter registration
- Arizona maintains bifurcated system: DPOC for state/local, not for federal

Georgia database-check model:
- Uses DHS SAVE database cross-reference (not documentary proof at registration)
- Found 1,634 noncitizen registration attempts. All placed in pending status. None voted.
- 100% REAL ID compliant

Noncitizen voting evidence nationally:
- Heritage Foundation database: 77-99 confirmed cases, 1999-2025
- Iowa audit: initial claim 2,176, investigated → 35 actual voters (0.01%)
- Michigan 2024: 16 instances (0.00028% of votes)
- Georgia: 1,634 attempts caught, zero voted

## Academic Evidence

GAO-14-634 (2014): Kansas and Tennessee turnout declined 1.9-3.2 percentage points more than comparison states after voter ID implementation. Larger impact on 18-23 year olds, new registrants, African Americans.

NBER Working Paper 25522 (2019): 1.6 billion observations, 2008-2018. No statistically significant negative effect on registration or turnout for any demographic group. Also found no effect on fraud.

Lipkovitz (2025): Late-adopting states see 2.7pp turnout decrease. Early-adopting states show no significant effect — suggesting adaptation over time.

Note: All academic studies examined photo voter ID laws, NOT documentary proof of citizenship requirements. DPOC is more burdensome than photo ID.
