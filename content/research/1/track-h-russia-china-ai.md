# Track H: Russia-China AI and Technology Cooperation
**Research Track:** Supplementary -- Russia-China AI/Technology Cooperation
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Researcher:** Grunt

## Executive Summary: Questioning the Core Assumptions

The prevailing narrative -- "Russia and China are strategically cooperating on AI to challenge US dominance" -- is mostly theater. The reality: Russia is a declining AI power desperately leeching off China's tech ecosystem while China pursues independent development. This isn't a partnership. It's a dependency relationship that China tolerates for geopolitical reasons, not technological ones.

**Core findings:**
1. **Cooperation is performative, not substantive** -- Joint declarations abundant, joint research minimal (0.1% of China's AI output)
2. **Russia contributes almost nothing** -- Mathematics talent exodus, no compute infrastructure, 1.5-2 years behind Ukraine on battlefield AI
3. **China leads, Russia follows** -- DeepSeek code literally forms the basis of Russia's "domestic" GigaChat MAX model
4. **The AGI race framing is a distraction** -- Real competition is US-China; Russia isn't racing, it's scavenging
5. **Sanctions created dependency, not partnership** -- 88% of Russia's chips now from China, not because of AI cooperation but because China ignores sanctions
6. **Brain drain gutted Russian AI** -- 50,000-70,000 IT professionals fled; those who left were responsible for 20% of Russia's international collaborations

## Assumption Challenge #1: Is Russia-China AI Cooperation Real?

**ASSUMPTION:** Russia and China are jointly developing AI capabilities to challenge the West.

**REALITY:** They announced cooperation in January 2025 with a [Joint Declaration on Cooperation in AI](https://valdaiclub.com/a/highlights/development-of-artificial-intelligence-in-eurasia/), including plans for joint research labs, student exchanges, and shared AI models. Putin ordered [Sberbank to work with China on AI](https://www.voanews.com/a/russia-turns-to-china-to-step-up-ai-race-against-us/7931829.html) in January 2025.

**BUT:** Research from Georgetown's Center for Security and Emerging Technology found that between 2010-2019, Chinese and Russian researchers collaborated on only [296 English-language AI papers -- less than 0.1% of China's AI output and about 2% of Russia's](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/headline-or-trend-line/). Private AI investment between the two countries totaled only $879 million across 12 deals from 2016-2019.

**Translation:** Lots of joint statements and photo ops. Almost no actual collaboration. [The gap between aspirations and reality is significant](https://www.afpc.org/publications/articles/a-new-sino-russian-high-tech-partnership).

**Why this matters:** Western analysts keep treating Russia-China AI cooperation as a strategic threat. The data shows it's mostly aspirational. China is developing AI independently and occasionally lets Russia copy their homework.

## Assumption Challenge #2: Russia Is a Real AI Competitor

**ASSUMPTION:** Russia is a player in the global AI race with military applications and strong domestic capabilities.

**REALITY CHECK -- Military AI:**
- By early 2025, Russian Ministry of Defence acknowledged they [hadn't fielded a fully functional unmanned systems management system](https://www.cnas.org/publications/commentary/roles-and-implications-of-ai-in-the-russian-ukrainian-conflict), putting them **1.5-2 years behind Ukraine**
- Russia announced [Unmanned Systems Forces (VBS) in December 2024](https://www.saratoga-foundation.org/p/russias-new-unmanned-systems-forces) as an institutional response to operational lessons from Ukraine
- They're developing [AI-driven drones (V2U) that use terrain analysis](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/russia-ukraine-war-ai-drones/) and operate without GPS
- But this is battlefield improvisation, not cutting-edge AI research

**REALITY CHECK -- Civilian AI:**
- Russian LLMs (Sberbank's GigaChat, YandexGPT) [rank 12th and 17th out of 44 models](https://www.rferl.org/a/deepseek-russian-ai-sber-yandex-kandinsky-censorship/33305704.html) on Chatbot Arena benchmarks
- Russia's "new" [GigaChat MAX model literally uses DeepSeek's code](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3297211/how-chinese-ai-start-deepseek-may-help-drive-innovation-countries-russia) as its basis
- [Russia lags behind China in AI investment and development speed](https://www.rferl.org/a/deepseek-russian-ai-sber-yandex-kandinsky-censorship/33305704.html)

**Translation:** Russia is not a competitor. It's a declining power desperately trying to stay relevant by copying Chinese models and improvising military AI under battlefield pressure.

**Why this matters:** US policy debates treat Russia as a peer AI adversary. It's not. Russia is 5-10 years behind in foundational AI research and getting further behind every quarter due to brain drain and sanctions.

## Assumption Challenge #3: The AGI Race Is Real

**ASSUMPTION:** There's a three-way race (US-China-Russia) to develop Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).

**REALITY:** The competition is [US-China, full stop](https://www.rand.org/pubs/perspectives/PEA4155-1.html). According to Trump's AI czar David Sacks, ["China is not years behind us in AI. Maybe they're three to six months"](https://time.com/7312305/agi-race-us-china-trump/). [DeepSeek's success shattered the assumption that the US maintained a comfortable lead](https://www.thecairoreview.com/essays/a-costly-illusion-of-control/).

Russia's role: [Trying to build cheap but powerful AI weapons and leveraging niche strengths in electronic warfare AI](https://www.rand.org/pubs/perspectives/PEA4155-1.html) to remain relevant.

**Translation:** The "AGI race" framing includes Russia by habit, not evidence. China and the US both have the compute and talent for next-generation models. Russia doesn't. Russia is trying to find niches where they can remain relevant (cheap autonomous weapons, military AI applications) while the real race happens between US and China.

**Why this matters:** Policy justifications for AI spending and regulation often invoke the "Russia-China threat." But if Russia isn't actually racing to AGI, the threat calculus changes. We're spending attention and resources on a two-player game while pretending it's three players.

## Assumption Challenge #4: What Does Russia Actually Contribute?

**ASSUMPTION:** Russia brings valuable assets to an AI partnership -- mathematics talent, cyber capabilities, energy for data centers.

**REALITY CHECK -- Mathematics Talent:**
- Russia historically strong: [programmers from MIPT, HSE, MGU, ITMO lead international Olympiads](https://www.afpc.org/publications/articles/a-new-sino-russian-high-tech-partnership)
- BUT: [50,000-70,000 IT professionals fled after Ukraine invasion](https://oilprice.com/Geopolitics/International/Russian-Tech-Talent-Exodus-Hinders-AI-Progress.html)
- [11% of Russian developers left by November 2022](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epjds/s13688-023-00389-3) -- these 11% had been responsible for **20% of Russia's international collaborations**
- [International collaborations with Russian scientists fell 34% by 2024](https://sldinfo.com/2025/09/putins-war-how-russias-invasion-of-ukraine-triggered-a-scientific-exodus/) compared to 2021

**REALITY CHECK -- Compute Infrastructure:**
- Zero. Russia has no domestic advanced chip production. [TSMC manufactured almost all Russian processors](https://carnegieendowment.org/russia-eurasia/politika/2023/07/vital-microchip-sanctions-will-hit-russian-computing-power-hard) (28nm technology) before sanctions cut them off.
- [88% of chips Russia acquired in H1 2023 came from China](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/cpus/nearly-90-of-chips-used-in-russia-come-from-china-despite-us-sanctions-report)
- Russia is buying Chinese chips that are themselves based on Western designs/equipment

**REALITY CHECK -- What Russia Actually Has:**
- Niche military AI applications (surveillance, autonomous weapons improvisation)
- [Cyber capabilities](https://sites.tufts.edu/hitachi/files/2021/02/1-s2.0-S0030438720300648-main.pdf) (offensive cyber operations, disinformation AI)
- Energy for data centers (but China doesn't need Russian energy for compute)
- Geographic/linguistic data for Eurasian markets

**Translation:** Russia used to have world-class mathematics talent. That talent left. Now Russia has energy, geography, and improvised military AI applications. None of these are strategically valuable to China's AI development. What Russia brings is geopolitical alignment and willingness to ignore Western norms, not technological capability.

**Why this matters:** Analysts frame Russia-China AI cooperation as if Russia is an equal partner contributing unique capabilities. The evidence shows Russia is a client state buying Chinese chips and copying Chinese models. This is a dependency relationship, not a partnership.

## Assumption Challenge #5: Sanctions Created a "Sanctioned-State Tech Ecosystem"

**ASSUMPTION:** Western sanctions pushed Russia, Iran, North Korea, and China into a coordinated technology ecosystem for AI and advanced tech.

**REALITY -- Russia-China Technology Dependency:**
- [China provides 88% of Russia's chips](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/cpus/nearly-90-of-chips-used-in-russia-come-from-china-despite-us-sanctions-report), but this is [sanctions evasion, not cooperation](https://www.defenseone.com/business/2024/09/big-loophole-allowing-russia-access-us-chips-china/399455/)
- Chinese distributors operate in a grey area, [continuing to supply Russian buyers with Western-designed chips](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/chinese-companies-allegely-ship-dual-use-equipment-to-russia-exposing-loopholes-in-western-sanctions/)
- [Chinese government turns a blind eye](https://www.aei.org/research-products/report/the-impact-of-semiconductor-sanctions-on-russia/) to these transactions

**REALITY -- Iran-Venezuela-Russia Pattern:**
- Similar to Iran's rial crisis and Venezuela's oil-for-gold deals, [Russia turned to China and Iran as alternative suppliers](https://www.csis.org/analysis/collaboration-price-russian-military-technical-cooperation-china-iran-and-north-korea) after sanctions
- BUT: This is not a coordinated "axis" -- it's **parallel bilateral dependencies**
- Iran needs Russian military tech and Chinese consumer goods
- Russia needs Chinese chips and Iranian drones
- North Korea provides artillery to Russia
- These are transactional relationships, not strategic coordination

**Translation:** There's no "sanctioned-state tech ecosystem" in the sense of coordinated technology development. What exists is a network of bilateral dependencies where China is the hub and other sanctioned states (Russia, Iran, North Korea) are spokes buying Chinese tech or trading with each other out of necessity, not strategy.

**Why this matters:** The framing of a "sanctioned-state tech ecosystem" implies strategic coordination that doesn't exist. This is important for policy: you can't disrupt an ecosystem that isn't coordinated. These are bilateral relationships that respond to different incentives.

## What Did Russia Actually Get From China?

Concrete evidence of technology transfer:

1. **Hardware:**
   - [88% of Russia's chips from China](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/cpus/nearly-90-of-chips-used-in-russia-come-from-china-despite-us-sanctions-report), mostly Western designs routed through Chinese distributors
   - [AI-enabled weaponry hardware](https://g2webcontent.z2.web.core.usgovcloudapi.net/OEE/TIP/T2COM_16DEC2025_CRINK_RUS_Benefitting_UKR_AI__anonymous.pdf) -- Chinese factories provide hardware and AI software for Russian drones
   - [4G data modems and Chinese satellite navigation antennas](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/russia-ukraine-war-ai-drones/) for Shahed drones

2. **Software/Models:**
   - [DeepSeek code forms basis of GigaChat MAX](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3297211/how-chinese-ai-start-deepseek-may-help-drive-innovation-countries-russia)
   - Partnership projects with [Qwen-3, DeepSeek, and MiniMax models](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/headline-or-trend-line/)
   - Open-source models Russia can copy without coordination

3. **Institutional:**
   - [Joint university partnerships](https://global.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202508/23/WS68a9537ca310851ffdb4fb95.html) (Harbin Institute of Technology + Bauman Moscow State Technical University)
   - [Proposed joint research labs](https://valdaiclub.com/a/highlights/china-russian-cooperation-in-the-ai-era/)
   - Joint data collection/labeling agreements

**But:** These institutional partnerships have produced minimal output. The real transfer is hardware (chips) and software (open-source models Russia copies).

## The Real Question: Why Does China Tolerate This?

China gains almost nothing technologically from Russia. So why maintain the appearance of cooperation?

**Plausible reasons:**
1. **Geopolitical alignment** -- Russia as a partner against US hegemony is valuable
2. **Buffer state** -- Russia absorbs Western attention and resources
3. **Market for Chinese tech** -- Russia is a captive buyer of Chinese chips, surveillance tech, consumer goods
4. **Energy security** -- Russia provides oil/gas, though China is diversifying
5. **Diplomatic cover** -- China can point to Russia partnership when accused of authoritarian tech development

**Translation:** This isn't about AI cooperation. It's about China maintaining a geopolitically useful relationship with a declining power that needs Chinese technology. Russia gets to claim it's still relevant in AI. China gets a partner that votes with them at the UN and buys their chips.

## Conclusion: Challenge the Framing

**The assumption:** "Russia and China are cooperating on AI to challenge US dominance."

**The reality:** Russia is a declining AI power dependent on Chinese technology, copying Chinese models, and buying Chinese chips. China is developing AI independently and occasionally letting Russia use their open-source work for geopolitical reasons, not technological ones.

**The AGI race:** It's US-China. Russia is not racing. Russia is scavenging.

**The sanctioned-state ecosystem:** There is no coordinated ecosystem. There are bilateral dependencies where China is the hub and other sanctioned states are desperate buyers.

**What Russia contributes:** Geography, energy, cyber capabilities, and geopolitical alignment. Not foundational AI research. Not compute infrastructure. Not talent (that talent left).

**Why this matters for Signal Dispatch analysis:**

If the US is distracted by Iran-Venezuela pressure campaigns, does that create an opening for Russia-China AI cooperation? **No.** Because that cooperation doesn't really exist in a meaningful form.

Does it create an opening for China to accelerate independent AI development? **Maybe.** But China is already developing AI independent of US-China relations. The real question is whether US distraction changes China's calculus on Taiwan, not whether Russia becomes an AI power.

**The bottom line:** Stop treating Russia as a peer AI competitor. It's not 2015 anymore. The brain drain, sanctions, and Ukraine war have gutted Russia's AI capabilities. Russia is riding China's coattails and improvising military AI under battlefield pressure. The threat is China, not Russia. The cooperation is theater, not substance.
