# SD #11 Cross-Track Intelligence Synthesis
## Democratic Faith in New York State — Erosion, Governance, and Dealignment

*Phase 4A — Liara synthesis across 12 research agents, 3 rounds*

---

## The Core Thesis

**New York Democrats are winning elections they are losing the argument for.**

The data across all twelve tracks tells a single coherent story: the Democratic Party in New York State has achieved a genuinely impressive policy record (congestion pricing, paid leave, good cause eviction, free meals, minimum wage, childcare), maintained organizational dominance, and benefited from Republican weakness — while simultaneously hemorrhaging registrations, trust, and residents. The party is structurally healthy by every institutional measure and structurally sick by every populist one. It controls the government and cannot solve the problem that defines its voters' daily lives.

This is not a story about left vs. center, progressive vs. moderate, or upstate vs. downstate. It is a story about a governing party that has become trapped between its donor class and its voter base on the single issue that matters most. Real estate lobbied $13.6M and gave Hochul $4.8M personally. The affordable housing numbers have a credibility gap (65K claimed vs 38-47K verifiable). Rent is $4,929 a month. Homelessness doubled. 137K people left. The party passed substantial legislation on everything *except* the thing that would require confronting the interests that fund it. Agenda control — the ability of leadership to prevent popular bills from reaching a floor vote even when they have majority co-sponsors — is the mechanism.

The 2026 cycle will be shaped by three colliding forces: the federal fiscal crisis ($13.5B annual losses, 470K losing healthcare) creating an external threat; a primary insurgency (DSA's largest-ever slate, AOC leading Schumer by 19pts) from the base; and a budget deadline (April 1, $7.1B gap, no deal) forcing choices between austerity and confrontation with the donor class.

---

## Signal-by-Signal Interpretation

### Registration
-550K Democrats in three years. Unaffiliated at 25.24% statewide, surpassing Republicans. But destination is blank, not Republican — exit without defection. 2025 local wave shows voters remain available under certain conditions. Conditional withdrawal, not permanent departure.

### Polls & Trust
Democratic favorability at 34% nationally (all-time low). 86% say NY unaffordable. 67% of Democrats say their party frustrates them. Hochul wins by default (57% wanted someone else). Healthcare trust advantage (49-26 over GOP) about to be stress-tested by 470K Essential Plan losses.

### Elections
Genuinely contradictory. 2024 presidential bad (margin halved). 2025 local excellent (historic county flips). Different elections measure different things — presidential = national brand; local = organizational capacity + candidate quality. Democrats losing the brand war while winning the ground game.

### Policy
Objectively substantial record. But none of it touches the dominant voter concern. NYC rent $4,929/month. Homelessness doubled. 137K left. The one thing that defines daily life — cost — remains unsolved.

### Money
Two stories in opposite directions. Grassroots historic highs (ActBlue +41%, Mamdani $17.2M on $99 avg). Institutional collapse (DNC $12.6M + $17M debt vs RNC $90M). Outside spending hostile ($700M+ right-leaning vs ~$80M progressive).

---

## Cross-Signal Convergence

**Strongest convergence: Affordability as master variable.** Every track points to the same center. Registration losses, poll numbers, policy invisibility, budget crisis, Latino shift, Schumer's 27%, outmigration — all trace back to cost of living. Twelve independent tracks, one center of gravity.

**Strongest divergence: Election results vs. everything else.** 2025 wave contradicts polls and registration. Reflects attitude vs. behavior gap — voters angry in abstract but vote for specific candidates who show up.

**Second convergence: Institutional blockage.** Build Public Renewables (83 sponsors, 76 needed — no floor vote). NY Health Act (sufficient co-sponsors — no floor vote). Real estate ($13.6M). The blocker is agenda control, not ideology.

**Third convergence: Dealignment without realignment.** Latino registrants: 43% Dem (from 55%), 42% unaffiliated (from 34%). Statewide: growth in no party. "Dealignment stuck. Realignment didn't."

---

## Net Directional Assessment

**Structural erosion masked by contextual advantages.**

The erosion is real and measurable across every demographic and geographic subgroup. The contextual advantages — Republican weakness, Trump mobilization, progressive candidate quality, federal overreach as external villain — are sufficient to win elections in the near term. Not sufficient to reverse the underlying erosion.

The critical distinction: **electoral viability vs. coalitional health.** Democrats will almost certainly hold power through 2026. But they will do so with a shrinking, frustrated base voting against the alternative rather than for the party. Winning elections while losing faith is sustainable for a cycle or two. Not indefinitely.
