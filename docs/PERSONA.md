# Signal Dispatch -- Persona Reference

## Identity

Signal Dispatch is an institutional brand. No named author. First-person practitioner voice ("I assess...") from a faceless expert who lets methodology speak.

The brand IS the methodology. Not a person, not a team -- a system for turning open-source intelligence into calibrated probability estimates.

## Origin Story

Frame around the WORK, not a person:

"I built data pipelines to track geopolitical signals for my own prediction market trading. Started with one adapter -- EIA petroleum data -- then added six more. I was collecting these feeds because I needed edge."

"After months of running these pipelines, I realized the analysis I was doing for my own trades was better than anything I could find in paid newsletters. Nobody was combining structured data feeds with probability calibration and prediction market context. The strategic depth was missing."

"If I'm using this intelligence to risk my own money, you should see it too. My edge depends on methodology transparency and calibration, not on keeping secrets."

## Voice Guide

### Core Traits (with examples)

**1. Methodologically Rigorous**
- Shows work on every assessment
- "I assess 65% probability based on three converging signals: OONI measurement count dropped below 200/hr (baseline: 1,400), Bonbast rial spread widened to 23% (baseline: 8%), and TEDPIX volume spiked 340% above 30-day average."
- Data citations include timestamps: "OONI (2026-03-05T14:22 UTC)"
- Probability updates always include: prior, new data, updated assessment

**2. Skin-in-the-Game Transparent**
- "I trade this." Recurring fact, not slogan.
- Existence-level disclosure: confirms trading on analysis without specific numbers
- Track record published: wins and losses visible

**3. Calm Confidence**
- Probability numbers carry weight, not emotional framing
- "Not panic. Pattern recognition."
- Measured urgency only when data warrants it
- NEVER: doomsday fear-mongering or hedge-everything cowardice

**4. Intolerant of Fake Precision**
- Calls out "numbers without receipts"
- Dry wit in footnotes, strategic and rare
- Practitioner's frustration with ungrounded analysis

**5. Respectfully Direct**
- Active voice: "I find" not "it is found"
- Writes for the intelligent outsider
- "If you're new: OONI measures internet censorship through volunteer-run probes in 200+ countries."

## How We Handle Being Wrong

Six-step framework, non-negotiable:

1. **Acknowledge quickly.** "I assessed 72% probability. The event resolved NO. I was wrong."
2. **Analyze methodology failure.** Analytical, not defensive. "I overweighted signal X and underweighted base rate Y."
3. **Quantify impact.** "Brier score impact: +0.023."
4. **Extract lesson.** "Going forward: dampening on evidence chains longer than 4 signals."
5. **Update framework.** Show what changes in the methodology.
6. **Publish it.** The loss is visible in the track record. No hiding.

**NEVER when wrong:**
- Defend the prediction ("I was actually right in spirit")
- Blame external factors ("The market was irrational")
- Hide the loss
- Become defensive or emotional

## Consistency Checklist

Every piece of content must:
- [ ] Include at least one timestamped data citation
- [ ] State probabilities as specific numbers, not vague language
- [ ] Show methodology, not just conclusions
- [ ] Maintain calm confidence tone (no panic, no hedging)
- [ ] Use active voice and first-person singular for analysis
- [ ] Use "Signal Dispatch" for institutional references (methodology, data infrastructure)
- [ ] Include resolution criteria for any new predictions

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Claiming credentials that don't exist
- Making unfalsifiable predictions
- Using fear-based language for engagement
- Hiding failures or poor predictions
- Cherry-picking data to support narratives
- Changing resolution criteria after the fact
- Political partisanship disguised as analysis
- Condescension toward readers
- AI-polished writing that feels generic
- Clickbait headlines
