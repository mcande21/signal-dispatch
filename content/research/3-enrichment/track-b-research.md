# WEB RESEARCH

## 1A. Fertilizer Supply Chain Through Hormuz

**Commodities and volumes transiting Hormuz monthly:**

- **Urea:** 1.5-1.9 million tonnes/month from Arab Gulf + Iran. Qatar and Iran ~5mt annually; UAE and Saudi Arabia ~2mt more. 35-45% of globally traded urea exits through Hormuz.
- **Ammonia:** 400-500 kt/month. Gulf states ~30% of global ammonia exports. Gas-to-ammonia production concentrated in Qatar and Saudi Arabia.
- **Sulphur:** 1.5-1.8 million tonnes/month. ~45% of global sulphur exports transit Hormuz. Critical input for phosphate processing (Morocco, India).
- **DAP/MAP (phosphates):** 400-500 kt/month. ~18% of global MAP/DAP trade from Gulf states.

**Compounding mechanism:** Natural gas is primary feedstock for ammonia and nitrogen fertilizers. Hormuz closure simultaneously blocks (a) finished fertilizer exports, (b) LNG that feeds fertilizer plants elsewhere, (c) sulphur that feeds phosphate processing. QatarEnergy stopped urea and ammonia production when it halted LNG output on March 2.

**Key exporters affected:** Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iran (world's second-largest urea supplier), Bahrain, Oman.

## 1B. EU Gas Storage Refill Math

**Current position:** EU gas storage at ~30% of capacity as of late Feb/early March 2026. Total capacity ~105 bcm. Current inventory roughly 31-32 bcm (briefing cited 46 bcm -- AGSI data will resolve).

**Target:** 90% fill by Oct-Dec 2026 = ~94.5 bcm. Effective floor with flexibility: 80-85% (~84-89 bcm).

**Gap:** Starting from ~30-46 bcm, need to inject 48-65 bcm March-November. 5-year average injection rate for March-October: ~55-60 bcm.

**Can Europe hit 90% without Qatari LNG?**
- Qatar's direct share: 12-14% of EU LNG imports = ~15-18 bcm/year, ~7-10 bcm during injection season
- US LNG can partially backfill (58% of EU LNG imports). Golden Pass (18.1 mtpa) expected Q1 2026, but QatarEnergy ownership complicates.
- Global LNG supply growing +50 bcm in 2026, but Asian competition fierce
- German FSRU capacity rising to 4.6 bcm in 2026-2027

**Assessment:** 90% achievable but requires sustained high TTF prices, max Norwegian pipeline, demand destruction. 5-10 bcm shortfall plausible, pushing toward flexibility provisions (80-85%). Real risk is not the target -- it's the price. TTF already EUR 55-60/MWh and spiking.

## 1C. Price-to-Plate Transmission Timeline

1. **Immediate (0-2 weeks):** Fertilizer spot prices spike. Already happened -- urea up 20% in 48h regionally, 6.5% globally first 10 days of March.
2. **Planting decisions (now through April):** Binary choice -- absorb costs or reduce application. Under-application more likely for cash-constrained operations.
3. **Yield impact (August-October 2026):** Reduced application cuts yields. Estimates: 2-5% yield reduction on staple crops.
4. **Food price transmission (September 2026 - Q1 2027):** Reduced harvest -> higher commodity prices -> higher retail food prices.

**The 6-month fuse is accurate:** March fertilizer shock -> Aug-Oct yield shortfall -> Sep-Dec food price spike.

**Critical detail:** Supply-side shock with built-in delay. Markets may not price food impact until harvest data arrives Q3. Information asymmetry window.

## 1D. Countries Most Exposed

**Tier 1 -- Critical dependency:**
- **India:** Gulf provides ~half its urea + significant phosphate precursors. Double exposure: finished imports + LNG feedstock for domestic plants.
- **Brazil:** Imports ~90-100% of urea, 40%+ transiting Hormuz. 7.5-8.5mt/year. World's largest soybean exporter.

**Tier 2 -- High exposure:**
- **Sub-Saharan Africa:** Already low fertilizer usage. Price increases reduce application further on food-insecure continent.
- **Morocco:** Depends on Gulf sulphur/ammonia for phosphate fertilizer production. Major phosphate exporter -- disrupts downstream globally.
- **Southeast Asia:** Major urea importers from Gulf.

**Tier 3 -- Significant but manageable:**
- **United States:** Ammonia 10% of imports; processed phosphates (MAP, DAP, TSP) 40% of imports. Domestic production buffer.
- **Europe/Latin America:** Significant urea volumes from Middle East.

## 1E. Alternative Routes and Costs

**Cape of Good Hope reroute:**
- +$1M per voyage in fuel
- +2-3 weeks transit (Gulf to Europe)
- Emergency surcharges: ~$60/TEU standard, ~$90/TEU reefer (MSC)
- Already congested from Houthi-related Suez avoidance

**Pipeline alternatives for gas:**
- Norwegian capacity at/near maximum
- Algeria pipeline to Spain/Italy provides some diversification
- No pipeline alternative for Gulf LNG -- fundamental vulnerability

**Cost amplification:** EUR weakness compounds dollar-denominated energy/fertilizer import costs.

## 1F. Strongest Counterarguments

1. **SPR and strategic reserves:** G7 considering 60-100mb coordinated release. US SPR at 415mb. Impact: $10-20/bbl temporary. **BUT:** SPR addresses oil, not LNG or fertilizer. No meaningful strategic gas/fertilizer reserves exist.

2. **Growing LNG supply:** +10% in 2026 (446 to 490 million mt). **BUT:** Priced into pre-crisis expectations. Doesn't replace 19% near-term reduction from Qatar shutdown.

3. **Demand elasticity:** European industrial demand destruction occurring. Asian buyers pivot to spot. **BUT:** Demand destruction IS the economic damage.

4. **Chinese/Russian alternative supply:** China major fertilizer producer. Russia has nitrogen capacity. **BUT:** Chinese exports restricted. Russian plants hit by Ukrainian drones. Neither available at scale in 2026.

5. **Conflict resolution (STRONGEST):** Hormuz closures historically measured in weeks. Diplomatic pressure from all parties. **IF strait reopens within 4-6 weeks, fertilizer shock severe but not catastrophic. The 6-month fuse only detonates if disruption persists through planting season.**

# SIGNAL INTERPRETATION FRAMEWORK

## AGSI (European Gas Storage)
Storage is the structural vulnerability metric. At 30% fill (~31 bcm) with 90% mandate (94.5 bcm) by November, Europe needs ~60+ bcm injection in 7 months. Without Qatari LNG (7-10 bcm during injection season), refill math tightens from comfortable to requiring sustained high prices and demand destruction.

**Signal vs noise:** Day-to-day 0.1-0.3% fluctuations are weather noise. Sustained deviation from 5-year average injection trajectory of >5 percentage points = structural signal. Critical threshold: 80% by October. Falling below trajectory by August = genuine crisis. Combine with ENTSOG: lagging storage + declining pipeline imports = supply-driven shortfall.

## ENTSOG (Pipeline Flows)
Real-time supply picture underneath storage numbers. With Qatari LNG offline, pipeline dependencies (Norway, Algeria, residual Russian) become critical. Norwegian flow data (Langeled, Europipe, Franpipe) shows if Norway is maxing out. German data shows demand destruction. Italian data reveals Mediterranean LNG regas throughput. Polish data shows Baltic Pipe activity.

**Signal vs noise:** Normal daily variation 5-10%. Sustained decline >15% at major interconnection for 3+ days = supply disruption. Key diagnostic: if LNG terminal send-out drops while TTF rises = confirmed physical supply loss. Norwegian flows flat while LNG drops = ceiling hit on primary alternative. ENTSOG decline + AGSI injection shortfall = confirmed supply crisis.

## ECB (Exchange Rates)
Financial transmission mechanism. Energy imports are dollar-denominated. Weakening euro amplifies every barrel, cargo, and tonne Europe imports. EUR/GBP provides control: both weakening = euro-specific (energy capital flight); only EUR/USD = dollar strength.

**Signal vs noise:** Daily 0.5% moves = normal. Cumulative >3% over 2 weeks in energy crisis context = structural European vulnerability pricing. >5% from pre-crisis = energy-driven capital flight. Combine with AGSI: lagging storage + weakening euro = vicious cycle.

## Comtrade (Trade Flows)
Structural dependency baseline. Shows sourcing concentration and diversification progress. This is "pre-existing condition" data.

**Signal vs noise:** Inherently lagged (monthly/quarterly). Not real-time crisis indicator. Value is establishing: What % of German fertilizer imports transit Hormuz? How diversified is LNG import base? QoQ changes >20% in sourcing = active restructuring. Comtrade = vulnerability. AGSI + ENTSOG = whether vulnerability is being exploited.

## GDELT (Media Coverage)
Narrative momentum and market awareness proxy. Core thesis: media tracking energy, fertilizer, food separately. GDELT tests directly: independent coverage spikes = siloed framing. Convergence in same articles = market connecting dots.

**Signal vs noise:** Baseline fluctuation 20-30% daily. Sustained >200% above 30-day baseline for 3+ days = narrative breakout. Critical diagnostic: convergence vs divergence of three narratives. Mainstream outlets (FT, Bloomberg, Reuters) explicitly linking all three = information asymmetry window closing.

## Cross-Signal Diagnostic Chain
1. ENTSOG (leading): Supply disruption appears first
2. AGSI (accumulating): Storage trajectory diverges over days/weeks
3. ECB (amplifying): Euro weakness compounds import costs
4. Comtrade (structural): Confirms dependency magnitude
5. GDELT (awareness): Narrative convergence = markets pricing compounding effect

**Thesis-confirming:** ENTSOG down + AGSI below trajectory + EUR weakening + high Gulf dependency + GDELT narratives siloed = compounding crisis real but not yet priced.
**Thesis-disconfirming:** ENTSOG stable + AGSI on track + EUR stable + GDELT converging prematurely = crisis manageable, 6-month fuse defused.
