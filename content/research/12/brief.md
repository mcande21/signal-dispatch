# Signal Dispatch #12 -- Research Brief

## Executive Summary

The legal architecture of American electricity regulation contains a structural gap between retail obligation to serve (absolute) and wholesale obligation to serve (contractual, discretionary). NV Energy is exploiting this gap to terminate a 16-year wholesale power supply agreement with Liberty Utilities, cutting off 49,000 Lake Tahoe residents by May 2027. The proximate cause: data centers now account for 78% of capacity requests in NV Energy's territory. The capacity freed by terminating Liberty will serve TRIC data center projects. Residential ratepayers fund the $4.2B Greenlink West transmission line, of which 4,000 of 5,000 MW is committed to data centers.

FERC has jurisdiction but has not been invoked by any party. NV Energy filed a PPA amendment at FERC in November 2025 — four months before public disclosure — in a routine combined notice that received zero protests. The regulatory gap is structural, but the attention gap is what makes it actionable.

This is not a Nevada anomaly. At least 10 states have active conflicts. PJM capacity prices jumped 11x. NERC issued a Level 3 alert. Community moratoriums are spreading nationally.

## Thesis (Cooper-approved, three moves)

1. **The mechanism is legal.** Obligation to serve dissolves at wholesale. NV Energy chose not to renew a temporary contract.
2. **The choice is economic.** Data centers made the capacity more valuable. Ratepayers subsidize the infrastructure. Working-class communities absorb the cost.
3. **The absence is regulatory.** FERC has tools but nobody has used them. The money and attention flow one direction.

## Key Numbers (Legion-calibrated)

- 78% of capacity REQUESTS are data centers (40-60% after NV Energy discounting = actual planning load)
- 0 MW available import capacity in Sierra Pacific territory
- $4.2B Greenlink West: 4,000 of 5,000 MW committed to data centers, funded by residential ratepayers
- $457M in tax incentives for ~300 permanent data center jobs ($1.5M/job)
- 49,000 affected residents; South Lake Tahoe is 26.1% Hispanic/Latino, 50.7% earning <$49K/year
- 77% rate increase since late 2022 (mostly Liberty's own costs per Legion; data-center-driven shock comes post-May 2027)
- PJM capacity prices: $28.92 to $329.17/MW-day (~11x) across 13 states — different mechanism than Nevada
- NERC Level 3 alert (May 4, 2026) — highest severity
- November 17, 2025: FERC amendment filed; zero protests during 21-day comment period

## Legion Corrections to Incorporate

1. Use "78% of capacity requests" not "78% of load growth" — actual planning incorporation is 40-60%
2. Liberty may not be an innocent victim — Algonquin profits from higher replacement costs (higher rate base = higher returns)
3. NV Energy's strongest defense (contract was always temporary, Liberty had 16 years) is legitimate — the piece should acknowledge it, then show where it breaks (Greenlink allocation, marketing contradiction)
4. PJM 11x is a different market mechanism than Nevada's bilateral termination — don't conflate
5. The 77% rate increase is mostly Liberty's own costs, not data-center-driven yet

2. **The Gap:** The wholesale/retail distinction. What obligation to serve means and where it stops.
