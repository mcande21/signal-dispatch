### Theme 1: Active War -- US-Israeli Strikes on Iran (Feb 28 - Present)

The single most important fact for this analysis: as of March 5, 2026, Iran is in an active shooting war with the United States and Israel.

**Timeline of escalation:**
- Late 2025: Indirect US-Iran nuclear negotiations via Oman mediation
- February 6, 2026: Talks in Muscat, Oman. Second round scheduled for Geneva
- February 27: Oman's Foreign Minister announces a "breakthrough" -- Iran agreed to halt enrichment stockpiling and accept full IAEA verification
- February 28: Hours after the diplomatic "breakthrough," Operation Roaring Lion (Israel) / Operation Epic Fury (US) begins. Joint strikes hit Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, Kermanshah
- February 28: Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei assassinated in strike on his compound. Multiple senior officials killed
- March 1-4: Iran retaliates with drones and ballistic missiles against Israel and US bases in Jordan, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Iraq, Saudi Arabia. UK bases in Bahrain, Qatar, and Cyprus also hit
- March 3-4: Continued strikes against nuclear sites at Natanz, missile production infrastructure, mobile launchers
- March 5: Iran's FM Araghchi rejects ceasefire negotiations, citing bad faith after strikes during active talks

**Key data points:** Strikes have destroyed nuclear enrichment-linked facilities at Natanz, missile production infrastructure, mobile launcher systems. No radiological release reported at Isfahan nuclear site.

Sources: UK House of Commons Library, Al Jazeera timeline, CBS News

### Theme 2: Economic Collapse and the Protest Cycle (Dec 2025 - Feb 2026)

The war arrived on top of an economy already in freefall. The internal crisis preceded and compounded the external one.

**Economic indicators:**
- Rial hit record low of 1,750,000/USD in December 2025 (black market). Current March 2026 rates show continued weakness around 1,315,000-1,666,000 range depending on market tier
- Year-on-year inflation at 42.2% (Iran statistics). IMF projects 43%+, placing Iran 4th globally behind Venezuela, Sudan, Zimbabwe
- World Bank projects GDP contraction in both 2025 and 2026
- 2 million additional people pushed below poverty line in 2025; poverty rate forecast to rise from 33.2% to 35.4%
- Iranian Central Bank chief resigned

**Protest timeline:**
- December 28, 2025: Protests begin among shopkeepers (bazaaris) -- economic grievances
- January 8-9, 2026: Regime implements internet shutdown and violent crackdown. Casualty estimates range from 7,015 confirmed deaths (HRANA) to 30,000-36,500 (contested)
- Mid-January: First wave of protests largely suppressed
- January 18: Partial internet restoration observed but heavily degraded
- February 21: Second wave -- student-led protests at multiple universities

Sources: Al Jazeera, Wikipedia 2025-2026 Iranian protests, NPR

### Theme 3: Sanctions Architecture and Its Effectiveness

Sanctions are no longer the primary pressure mechanism -- they have been superseded by direct military action. But the sanctions architecture created the economic conditions that enabled everything else.

**Key developments:**
- September 2025: UN Iran nuclear deal sanctions restored, triggered by UK, France, and Germany citing "significant non-performance"
- Sanctions have demonstrably halved the rial's value (July 2024 to March 2025) and driven inflation above 40%
- China continues purchasing most Iranian oil exports despite sanctions, alongside Russia refusing to recognize restored sanctions
- Sanctions enforcement gap: compliance from Western-aligned nations is high, but Iran's actual trade has shifted to China/Russia axis

**Analytical note:** The causal chain from sanctions to instability is well-documented: sanctions reduce oil revenue, rial depreciates, inflation rises, purchasing power collapses, protest movements form.

Sources: UK House of Commons Library, PolitiFact, HSToday

### Theme 4: Strait of Hormuz -- The Energy Chokepoint

The war has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz to commercial traffic, though not through physical blockade.

**Mechanism:** Iran's retaliatory strikes on tankers and regional military targets have caused insurance companies to withdraw war risk coverage. Result: the strait is technically "open" but commercially closed. The insurance withdrawal does the work that a physical blockade would.

**Energy market impact:**
- Brent crude rose 10-13% in initial trading after strikes began (from ~$73 to ~$83/barrel)
- European natural gas prices nearly doubled: from EUR 30/MWh to peak of EUR 60/MWh
- Oil supertanker rates have surged as shipping avoids the strait
- Trump announced the US would insure tankers transiting the strait, which temporarily eased prices

**Scale:** The strait normally carries one-fifth of globally consumed oil. Its effective closure is a first-order energy security event.

Sources: Al Jazeera Hormuz, Kpler, CNBC

### Theme 5: Regime Survival Counterarguments

**Arguments for regime resilience:**
1. IRGC is not a single-leader pyramid. Revolutionary Guards are a sprawling institution with multiple power centers
2. Decentralized military doctrine. FM Araghchi confirmed Iranian military units shifted to "mosaic defense" -- independent, isolated, operating on pre-issued general orders
3. Succession planning existed. Khamenei reportedly named successors
4. No unified opposition. Protesters lack shared political platform or leadership
5. Calibrated repression. Regime avoided scale of 2019 while still containing mobilization
6. Rally-round-the-flag. External military attack historically consolidates domestic support

**Arguments against resilience:**
1. Unprecedented convergence. Economic collapse + massive protests + external military strikes + leadership assassination has no historical precedent
2. Loss of proxy network. Assad's fall in 2024. Hezbollah and Houthi capabilities degraded
3. Legitimacy crisis. "A political order that has lost its capacity to adapt"
4. Coercive-dependent stability is inherently fragile

Sources: Stimson Center, Christian Science Monitor, Real Instituto Elcano
