## 1. Pattern Detection

### 1A. Native-Born Documentation Burden: Liara's Largest Blind Spot

Liara's synthesis anchors to naturalized citizen suppression. But the SAVE Act requires proof from EVERYONE — native-born included. The native-born channel is far larger:

- **New Hampshire:** 23,749 native-born citizens face documentation barriers (at 2.1% CDCE rate) vs 2,344 naturalized citizens (at 2.9%). The native-born burden is 10x the naturalized burden.
- **WI-3:** Only 18,018 naturalized citizens (3.0%) — Liara correctly notes this is low. But 582,776 native-born citizens × 2.1% = 12,238 facing friction. For a district decided by <12,000 votes, this is potentially determinative.

The native-born documentation axis (rural, elderly, poor, birth-certificate-dependent) cross-cuts partisan lines differently from the naturalized citizen axis.

### 1B. Re-Verification Trigger: Largest Unquantified Channel

Existing registrations are NOT grandfathered. Any "registration transaction" triggers re-verification. USPS: ~27M address changes annually.

- Per CD per 2-year cycle: ~124,138 address changes
- Assuming 65% are registered voters: ~80,690 re-verification triggers
- At 9.1% CDCE rate × 50% adaptation: **~3,671 voters lost per CD**
- This is **1.7x Liara's 2,220 estimate**

Re-verification affects current registered voters, not just new registrants. Telling someone who has voted for 20 years they need to re-prove citizenship is a qualitatively different political event.

### 1C. CPS PES3 Hidden Documentation Burden

PES3=3 (explicit documentation barrier): 646,508 weighted.
PES3=8 ("did not know how to register"): contains 788,929 naturalized citizens.
PES3=9 ("other reason"): contains 631,385 naturalized citizens.
Combined residual: 1,420,314 — **8.3x the explicit code.**

Under a prospective DPOC regime that didn't exist when surveyed, many PES3=8 and PES3=9 respondents would face documentation barriers. The CPS baseline significantly underestimates prospective DPOC burden.

### 1D. Statistical Anomalies in Census CD Data

Bottom CDs by naturalized share include competitive-adjacent rural districts:
- WI-7 (2.5%), MI-2 (2.2%), MI-1 (2.1%), MN-8 (2.1%)
- These are exactly where native-born documentation burden (birth certificate access, passport poverty) is maximally relevant.
- DPOC friction here falls disproportionately on Republican voters.

### 1E. Missing Data

Beyond CD-level passport data (Liara flags):
- Same-day registration rates by state/district (NH SDR = ~44K midterm voters, all needing physical docs at polls)
- Birth certificate possession rates by CD
- DHS SAVE database false positive rates by demographic
- Cross-state birth certificate request rates/fulfillment times

## 2. Contrarian Synthesis

### 2A. Impact Is LARGER Than Assessed

1. 2024 naturalized citizen realignment (51-47 Harris) may be an outlier. Prior four elections: 60-40+ Democratic. Single-cycle shift during anti-immigration election doesn't establish new baseline.
2. Liara's 2,220 per CD uses conservative assumptions. Kansas showed no declining trend in blocked citizens (31K over law's lifetime). 50% adaptation rate has no empirical anchor.
3. Re-verification channel adds 1.7x, skews more Democratic (movers are younger, more urban, more minority).
4. Independent suppression (13% CDCE rate × ~40% of electorate) dwarfs the naturalized citizen channel. In D+5-8 environment, suppressing Independents IS suppressing Democratic votes.
5. Base rate problem: SAVE database purges for near-zero-prevalence condition guarantees massive Type I errors (Kansas 800:1).

**Steel-man estimate: 4,000-8,000 net Democratic loss per competitive CD.** Margin-exceeding in 5-10 tightest House races.

### 2B. Impact Is NEGLIGIBLE (Political Theater)

1. Federal SAVE Act will not become law. P(law) < 5%. Reconciliation pathway structurally difficult.
2. State-level for 2026: only NH under litigation (30-40% implementation probability). No competitive federal races in WY, SD, UT.
3. NBER (1.6B observations): zero significant effect from voter ID on any demographic. Photo ID is less burdensome than DPOC, but null across 1.6B obs is a strong prior.
4. Kansas backlash: Davids won KS-3 during Kobach. Counter-mobilization is real.
5. Adaptation: NH signed in 2024 → 2 years of awareness + document campaigns by November 2026. Lipkovitz: early-adopters show no effect.

**Theater estimate: 200-800 net Democratic loss in NH only. Zero everywhere else for 2026.**

## 3. Probability Calibration

### P(Federal SAVE Act becomes law before 2026 midterms) = 2-4%
9 votes short of cloture. No reconciliation pathway. Trump's unrelated amendment demands make passage less likely.

### P(Net benefit to Republicans from DPOC) = 60-65%
NOT the 80-20 Liara's framing implies. Cross-cutting effects are genuinely close to balanced. Depends on Independents breaking anti-Trump AND 2024 naturalized realignment holding.

### Revised per-CD estimate: 2,000-5,000, central ~3,500
When re-verification channel is included. Uncertainty bounds: 500-8,000. Liara's 2,220 understates both central estimate and uncertainty.

### P(Mobilization backlash fully offsets suppression, at least one race) = 40-50%
P(fully offsets across all competitive races) = 15-25%. Backlash is partial and uncertain, not symmetric offset.

### P(NH HB 1569 blocked before Nov 2026) = 55-65%
Kansas + Arizona precedents favor plaintiffs. But current judiciary is more conservative. P(implemented for Nov 2026) = 35-45%.

### NH Senate mispricing: ~3 points
Market: D 84.5%. If HB 1569 implemented (40%): P(D) drops from ~88% to ~70-75%.
Blended: 0.6 × 0.88 + 0.4 × 0.72 = 81.6%. Mild mispricing, within normal noise for 20-month-out prediction.

## Summary for Shepard

Liara's synthesis is competent on the naturalized-citizen axis but systematically underweights three channels:
1. **Native-born documentation burden** — 10x larger than naturalized in NH, potentially determinative in WI-3
2. **Re-verification triggers** — 1.7x increase over Liara's estimate
3. **Hidden CPS barriers** — 8.3x undercount in residual categories

Revised: 3,000-5,000 per competitive CD (up from 2,220), bounds 500-8,000. Direction correct at 60-65% confidence.

"Only NH matters" is correct for 2026 but the NH impact is larger than Liara frames, primarily through same-day registration and native-born documentation channels she doesn't quantify.
