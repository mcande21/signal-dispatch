## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The November 17, 2025 FERC filing (ER26-534-000, "Amended Liberty PPA") and the December 3, 2025 companion filing (ER26-645-000, "SPPC-Harney Funding Agreement") appear to be the legal mechanism through which NV Energy formalized the May 2027 termination and restructured the end-of-life terms of the Liberty wholesale supply arrangement -- four months before any public disclosure.

**We cannot confirm the specific terms of the amendment** because the actual filing documents (FERC eLibrary accession 20251117-5192 and 20251203-5053) are accessible only through FERC's interactive eLibrary system, not through API or automated retrieval. However, the convergence of evidence from nine independent source types points strongly toward the amendment setting the firm May 2027 termination date.

## THE FILING: ER26-534-000

### What the Federal Register tells us

**Source:** 90 FR 52378 (November 20, 2025), Federal Register document 2025-20427, "Combined Notice of Filings #1"

Filing details (verbatim from the Federal Register combined notice):

- **Docket:** ER26-534-000
- **Applicant:** Sierra Pacific Power Company
- **Description:** "Sec. 205(d) Rate Filing: Filing of Amended Liberty PPA to be effective 12/29/2025"
- **Filed:** November 17, 2025
- **Accession Number:** 20251117-5192
- **Comment Deadline:** 5 p.m. ET December 8, 2025
- **Requested Effective Date:** December 29, 2025

### What the description tells us

The filing is classified as a Section 205(d) rate filing. Under 16 U.S.C. 824d(d), this section governs changes to rates, charges, classifications, and terms of service for jurisdictional sales. The use of "Amended Liberty PPA" rather than "Cancellation" or "Termination" means SPPC modified the existing agreement rather than ending it outright.

The effective date of December 29, 2025 is significant: it is the day before the current NV Energy Services Agreement's prior effective date pattern. The agreement chain runs:

| Period | Agreement | FERC Filings |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Jan 1, 2011 -- Dec 31, 2015 | Original PPA (5-year, full requirements) | RS 55 initial filing |
| Jan 1, 2016 -- Dec 30, 2020 | 2016 NV Energy Services Agreement | RS 55 1st & 2nd Amendments (ER15-2603, ER16-771) |
| Dec 30, 2020 -- [?] | 2021 NV Energy Services Agreement (current ESA) | RS 55 3rd Amendment (ER20-3017-000), SA 21-00011 (ER21-1476-000) |
| Dec 29, 2025 -- [May 2027?] | **Amended Liberty PPA** | **ER26-534-000** |

The December 29, 2025 effective date aligns perfectly with the pattern of prior agreement transitions. Each agreement iteration takes effect on or near December 30/January 1.

## THE RELATED FILING: ER26-645-000 (Harney Funding Agreement)

### What the Federal Register tells us

**Source:** 90 FR 56745 (December 8, 2025), Federal Register document 2025-22179, "Combined Notice of Filings #1"

Filing details (verbatim):

- **Docket:** ER26-645-000
- **Applicant:** Sierra Pacific Power Company
- **Description:** "205(d) Rate Filing: SPPC-Harney Funding Agreement Filing to be effective 12/4/2025"
- **Filed:** December 3, 2025
- **Accession Number:** 20251203-5053
- **Comment Deadline:** 5 p.m. ET December 24, 2025
- **Requested Effective Date:** December 4, 2025

### What is the Harney connection?

Harney Electric Cooperative (HEC) is an Oregon-based electric cooperative headquartered in Hines, OR, serving over 20,000 square miles in southeast Oregon and northern Nevada. Its service territory overlaps with SPPC's transmission system:

> "All power is purchased from the Bonneville Power Administration (BPA) under a long-term contract. Even though some power is received by HEC through Sierra Pacific Power Co.'s (SPPC) system in Nevada, it is purchased from BPA. BPA and SPPC have an agreement for providing power to HEC through SPPC's system."
-- Harney Electric Cooperative FAQ page

HEC operates as two systems: a north system connected directly to BPA, and a **south system located in and connected to NV Energy's service territory and Balancing Authority Area** (per BPA Rate Proceeding BP-26, petition to intervene).

The "SPPC-Harney Funding Agreement" likely governs the financial terms under which SPPC provides transmission/wheeling service for BPA power delivery to Harney Electric's Nevada-situated south system. The timing (filed 16 days after the Liberty PPA amendment) suggests it may be a restructuring of how SPPC handles third-party power delivery obligations as it winds down the Liberty wholesale supply arrangement.

**Assessment:** The Harney filing is most likely a separate but temporally related administrative matter -- SPPC restructuring its third-party delivery arrangements as the broader Liberty situation evolves. It is NOT a geographic reference to a project or substation; it refers to Harney Electric Cooperative specifically.

## THE CONTRACT CHAIN: WHAT WE NOW KNOW

### The original arrangement (2009-2011)

From Algonquin's 2011 SEC filing (Annual Report):

> "Liberty Utilities (West) has entered into a five year all requirements PPA with NV Energy to provide its full electric needs at NV Energy's 'system average cost' rates. The PPA had an effective starting date of January 1, 2011 with a five year renewal option."

Key terms of the original PPA:
- **Type:** Full requirements (NV Energy supplies 100% of needs minus self-generation)
- **Pricing:** NV Energy's "system average cost" rates
- **Initial Term:** 5 years (Jan 1, 2011 -- Dec 31, 2015)
- **Renewal:** Five-year renewal option
- **Renewables:** NV Energy required to use "commercially reasonable efforts" to supply sufficient renewable power to meet California's 20% RPS
- **FERC Rate Schedule:** SPPC Rate Schedule No. 55 (initially); later Rate Schedule No. 56
- **Contract Service Agreement:** 10-00625 (per FERC EQR data)
- **Contract Execution Date:** October 8, 2009

### First extension: 2016 NV Energy Services Agreement

- CPUC authorized in Decision D.15-12-021
- Liberty filed Application A.15-04-019 in April 2015 (the original PPA was about to expire Dec 31, 2015)
- Commenced January 1, 2016, expired December 30, 2020
- CPUC Advice Letter 51-E (December 22, 2015) implemented the amended agreement

### Second extension: 2021 NV Energy Services Agreement

- Commenced December 30, 2020
- Current ESA -- the one being amended/terminated
- CPUC Advice Letter 153-E implemented the 2021 agreement
- Under the 2021 ESA, NV Energy serves Liberty's "full energy requirements"
- Liberty can displace NV Energy RPS generation by procuring from its own solar projects (Luning, Turquoise)
- NV Energy required to deliver energy from Liberty's solar projects over NV Energy's transmission facilities at "FERC-regulated cost-based rates"

### FERC Rate Schedule evolution

FERC EQR (Electric Quarterly Report) transaction data confirms:
- **Rate Schedule:** FERC Electric SPPC Rate Schedule No. 56
- **Service Agreement:** 10-00625
- **Contract execution:** October 8, 2009
- **Trade date for current billing:** February 3, 2016
- **Recent billing rate:** $104.29/MWh
- **Classification:** Firm, Long-Term, Yearly increment
- **Balancing Authority:** NEVP
- **Service class:** ENERGY (full requirements)

### The November 2025 amendment: What it most likely changed

Based on convergence of evidence:

**From South Tahoe Now (March 22, 2026), quoting NV Energy director Katie Nannini:**
> NV Energy "agreed to keep providing service until Liberty's transmission service is available." The original "deadline for NV Energy to cease providing energy service" was **December 29, 2025**.

**From Fortune (May 12, 2026):**
> NV Energy spokesperson Katie Jo Collier characterized the transition as "rooted in a longtime understanding with Liberty well before data center load growth was a consideration," calling it "a planned transition for many years, not a reaction to recent developments." The agreement "was always intended to be temporary and transitional."

**From Energy News Beat (May 2026):**
> The arrangement was "extended in 2015, extended in 2020, extended again in late 2025" with "May 2027: Firm termination date established by NV Energy."

**Reconstruction:** The December 29, 2025 effective date in the FERC filing matches what South Tahoe Now reported as the original deadline for NV Energy to cease service. The 2021 ESA appears to have had an end date on or around December 29, 2025. When that date approached, instead of a new full extension, NV Energy agreed only to a bridge amendment -- extending service from December 29, 2025 to May 2027, with May 2027 as a firm, non-renewable termination date.

This is why the FERC filing is titled "Amended Liberty PPA" (extending/modifying the existing agreement) rather than a new service agreement or a cancellation.

## ASSESSMENT: WHAT DID THE AMENDMENT CHANGE?

### Hypothesis 1: Set a firm May 2027 termination date -- MOST PROBABLE (75%)

**Evidence for:**
- The December 29, 2025 effective date matches the reported original cessation deadline
- News sources confirm the arrangement was "extended again in late 2025" with May 2027 as firm end date
- The filing pattern matches: a Section 205(d) amendment that extends terms while adding a firm termination date
- NV Energy's public framing ("planned transition for many years") is consistent with a contractually agreed wind-down
- No cancellation or termination filing exists at FERC -- because the agreement was amended to include its own end date, not cancelled

**Evidence against:**
- We cannot read the actual filing document

### Hypothesis 2: Removed renewal/extension options -- PROBABLE (incorporated in H1) (15%)

**Evidence for:**
- The original PPA had a "five year renewal option" (per 2011 SEC filing)
- Prior extensions in 2016 and 2020 were exercised under renewal provisions
- Removing renewal options while setting a firm end date are logically complementary actions
- This would explain why the filing is an "amendment" rather than a new agreement

**Assessment:** Likely a component of Hypothesis 1 rather than a standalone change. The amendment probably both set the May 2027 end date AND eliminated any further renewal options.

### Hypothesis 3: Changed pricing or capacity terms to force Liberty out -- LOW PROBABILITY (5%)

**Evidence for:**
- The $104.29/MWh rate in FERC EQR data appears to be "system average cost" as originally contracted
- Making terms uneconomic would be one way to force a voluntary exit
- NV Energy has strategic incentive to reallocate capacity to higher-value data center customers

**Evidence against:**
- NV Energy has been publicly cooperative, agreeing to extend service beyond the December 2025 deadline
- The "system average cost" pricing structure is formula-based, not easily manipulated
- Liberty has not publicly complained about pricing changes
- This theory doesn't fit the "planned transition" framing

### Hypothesis 4: Routine/administrative amendment -- LOW PROBABILITY (5%)

**Evidence for:**
- FERC 205(d) amendments can be routine (updating contact information, minor term updates)

**Evidence against:**
- The timing is too precisely aligned with the public termination disclosure
- The December 29, 2025 effective date matches the reported cessation deadline
- A "routine" amendment would not warrant the title "Amended Liberty PPA" -- routine changes are typically described more specifically
- The companion Harney filing suggests a broader restructuring of SPPC's power delivery obligations

## WHAT WE STILL DON'T KNOW

1. **The actual text of the November 2025 amendment (ER26-534-000, accession 20251117-5192).** This is the single most important document. It would confirm whether the amendment set the May 2027 date, removed renewal options, or made other changes. Accessible via FERC eLibrary interactive browser search.

2. **Whether any party filed comments or protests before the December 8, 2025 deadline.** The comment period for ER26-534-000 was only 21 days. If no party protested, the amendment would have taken effect by operation of law.

3. **The relationship between the "Amended Liberty PPA" and the existing 2021 ESA.** Are these the same instrument? The FERC filing describes an amendment to the "Liberty PPA" while CPUC documents reference the "2021 NV Energy Services Agreement." These may be different names for the same FERC-jurisdictional agreement, or the FERC filing may govern different terms than the CPUC-approved ESA.

4. **When NV Energy internally decided to terminate.** The IRP filed in October 2024 identifies massive data center load growth. The FERC amendment was filed November 2025. The internal decision to terminate likely preceded the filing by months, possibly coinciding with IRP planning in mid-2024 or earlier.

5. **Whether Algonquin had a disclosure obligation.** If the amendment set a firm termination date for 75% of CalPeco's power supply, this could be material to Algonquin's financial outlook. The absence of any mention in Q3 2025 or Q4 2025 earnings materials raises questions about materiality assessment and timing of disclosure.

6. **The precise connection between the Liberty PPA amendment and the Harney Funding Agreement.** Filed 16 days apart, both involve SPPC restructuring its power delivery obligations. The Harney filing may represent SPPC reallocating transmission/delivery capacity previously committed to Liberty.
