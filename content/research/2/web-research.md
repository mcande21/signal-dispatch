# Web Research: Kristi Noem Fired as DHS Secretary

## Theme 1: Immediate Trigger -- Congressional Hearing Performance (March 3-4, 2026)

Noem's firing was triggered by her performance at two congressional hearings (March 3-4, 2026), where lawmakers from both parties expressed rare bipartisan hostility. The Senate Judiciary Committee hearing on March 4 was particularly damaging.

The final straw: Noem asserted during the Senate hearing that Trump had approved a $220 million DHS advertising campaign featuring herself on horseback. Trump immediately disputed this in a Thursday Reuters interview, stating he "never knew anything about it" and "wasn't thrilled" with the campaign. Republican senators reported this contradiction was the final trigger.

Trump announced Noem's removal via Truth Social on March 5, 2026, making her the first Cabinet member fired in his second term. She will step down March 31 and transition to a "special envoy for The Shield of the Americas" role.

## Theme 2: Alex Pretti Shooting and Narrative Collapse (January 24, 2026)

The fatal shooting of Alex Pretti, a 37-year-old ICU nurse and protester, occurred on January 24, 2026 in Minneapolis during an ICE enforcement operation.

Timeline:
- 8:54:54 a.m. -- Pretti arrives on Nicollet Avenue to observe ICE activity
- 9:00:50 a.m. -- A federal officer pepper-sprays Pretti
- 9:00:56 a.m. -- The officer pulls Pretti into the street
- 9:01:00 a.m. (approx) -- 10 shots fired in less than five seconds

Noem claimed Pretti "brandished his firearm and attacked ICE officers." Eyewitness videos contradicted this narrative: Pretti never reached for his gun, and one video shows a federal agent removing the weapon from Pretti's waist area just before he was shot.

Noem later described the scene as "chaotic" and said they "were using the best information we had at the time." The narrative collapse intensified scrutiny of DHS operational procedures and leadership judgment.

## Theme 3: The $220 Million Advertising Scandal

**Contract structure:** DHS awarded $220 million in taxpayer-funded contracts to two firms:
- People Who Think (Louisiana Republican ad firm): $77 million
- A mysterious Delaware LLC: $143 million

ProPublica investigation revealed a Noem-tied firm called "the Strategy Group" secretly received a piece of the contract but doesn't appear on public documents. The firm has "long-standing personal and business ties to Noem and her senior aides at DHS."

**Procurement bypass:** DHS invoked the border "emergency" to skip competitive bidding rules, tripling the agency's ad budget in one fiscal year.

**Political exposure:** The ads prominently featured Noem herself on horseback at Mount Rushmore. Bipartisan grilling during hearings -- Republican Senator John Kennedy and Democratic Congressman Joe Neguse both drew blood on this issue.

## Theme 4: Operational Failures and Staff Chaos

**FEMA dysfunction:** Noem bottlenecked Hurricane Helene disaster relief by requiring personal approval on all expenses over $100,000. She cycled through three acting FEMA administrators and presided over mass workforce cuts.

**Staff mismanagement:** Constant feuding across DHS components. Corey Lewandowski (political adviser) as "special government employee" with outsized influence. Incident where Lewandowski fired a pilot over a misplaced blanket became symbolic of internal chaos.

**Wrongful detentions:** Reports of children and families detained, wrongful detention of American citizens during ICE raids. Democratic lawmakers issued a 10-point reform demand list calling for judicial warrants, body cameras, mask bans, sensitive location protections, and state/local oversight.

## Theme 5: DHS Operational Impact -- Enforcement Continuity vs. Leadership Transition

**What doesn't change:** Core enforcement machinery continues. Under Noem, DHS removed 675,000 illegal aliens with an estimated 2.2 million self-deporting. Border crossings dropped 93% year-over-year. CBP delivered "zero releases" from May-November 2025. These are structural outcomes of Trump's directives, not Noem-specific.

**What might change:** Leadership tone and procedural oversight. Markwayne Mullin, Trump's nominee to replace Noem effective March 31, is a vocal immigration hardliner who has defended ICE agents, supported defunding sanctuary cities, called for completing the border wall, and championed "Remain in Mexico."

**Succession mechanics:** If Mullin is not confirmed by March 31, Deputy Secretary Troy Edgar (Navy veteran, former mayor) becomes acting secretary per statutory line of succession.

## Theme 6: Counterarguments and Defense of Noem's Tenure

**Official DHS defense highlights:**
- Over 3 million illegal aliens out of the country
- 82,000 applications for "Homeland Defenders" positions (agency record)
- Coast Guard recruitment at 121% of FY2025 target (5,204 new members, highest since 1991)
- FEMA recovered $8 billion in excess funds
- 70% of ICE arrests were criminal illegal aliens

**Strongest counternarrative:** What if Noem was fired for being *too effective*? The Pretti shooting was aggressive enforcement meeting predictable resistance. The advertising was visible deterrence. The FEMA cuts were fiscal discipline. In this frame, Noem was doing exactly what Trump wanted until the political heat became inconvenient.

If this holds, Mullin's tenure won't look different operationally. The firing becomes a lesson about political survival, not policy failure.
