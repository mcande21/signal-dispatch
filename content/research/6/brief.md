## Executive Assessment

The market at 28¢ is likely right. Powell will not say the word "recession" tomorrow.

This is not a macro call — it's a linguistics call. The resolution criteria require the verbatim word "recession" (or inflections/compounds). "Recessionary pressures" does not count. That single confirmed fact drives most of the analysis: Powell's entire rhetorical toolkit operates below the resolution threshold. He can say "significant softening," "considerable headwinds," "recessionary dynamics," and "extraordinary external circumstances" — none of it resolves YES.

The macro backdrop is objectively severe: -92,000 February NFP (worst jobs print in years), Q4 2025 GDP at 0.7%, unemployment in the 4.4–4.6% range. Goldman Sachs puts actual recession probability at 25%. But Powell has spent four years building an institutional practice of never saying the word — not at a press conference. He used it once, in June 2022, during congressional testimony where he couldn't choose his questioners. He has not used it in approximately 15 FOMC pressers since, through multiple cycles of deteriorating data.

The residual YES path is the denial mechanism: a reporter forces the word into the question so directly that Powell's deflection uses it. That path is real but thin — and rests on a poorly modeled conditional (how often reporters ask that exact framing vs. softer variants, at this specific type of event, with Powell in control of the room).

After adversarial review, the synthesis estimate of 34% compresses to 28–30%. The gap to market was approximately 6pp before adversarial pressure. It is now approximately 0–2pp. This is not a bet.

## Resolution Criteria

**Confirmed from Kalshi contract terms (High confidence):**

The contract resolves YES if the word **"recession"** (or its direct inflections: "recessions," "recession's") appears in Powell's spoken statements during the March 18, 2026 FOMC press conference.

**Does NOT count:**
- "Recessionary" (adjective form) — confirmed excluded
- "Recessionary pressures," "recessionary dynamics," "recessionary risk"
- Language in the written FOMC statement (only press conference spoken remarks)
- Language from other Fed officials who are not the presser speaker

**Does count:**
- Any direct usage, including denial: "I do not believe we are in a recession" — YES
- Any compound form that contains the base word

This is the central analytical fact. Powell's evasion vocabulary stays below the threshold by design.

## Historical Precedent

### Key Cases

**March 2020 — Emergency rate cut to zero, QE announced. Powell did not say "recession."**
The strongest analog. The most dramatic Fed action since 2008, announced in an emergency Sunday session. Powell held a presser the following week. Language deferred approximately six weeks. If he held the line then, the prior for holding the line tomorrow is strong.

**January 28, 2026 — Most recent presser. "Recession" absent from transcript.**
Confirmed via `FOMCpresconf20260128.pdf`. Conditions were already deteriorating pre-NFP. Powell maintained clean language. This is the verified behavioral baseline entering March 18.

**December 2025 — Prior KXFEDMENTION contract. Resolved NO at $0.01.**
Kalshi traders correctly priced this at near-zero during an already-softening environment.

**June 2022 — Powell said "recession" in congressional testimony.**
This is the primary YES precedent — and it's the wrong category. Congressional hearings are structurally different: senators control the frame, Powell cannot deflect without looking obstructionist, and he cannot choose his questioners. At an FOMC presser, he picks reporters, controls his time, and can pivot mid-sentence. The adversarial review rates this analog as inapplicable to presser dynamics. The actual presser base rate for Powell recession usage post-June 2022: **0 in approximately 15 pressers**, through multiple cycles of deteriorating conditions.

**January 9, 2008 — Bernanke analog.**
Pre-GFC, Bernanke hedged with "significant downside risks" and "possibility" language. The word itself deferred months into the recession.

### Powell's Documented Evasion Toolkit (5 patterns)
1. Epistemic disclaimer: "No one can predict with certainty..."
2. Data-dependency pivot: "We will monitor incoming data..."
3. Geopolitical attribution: "Extraordinary external circumstances..."
4. Institutional frame shift: "The Committee's assessment..."
5. Temporal deflection: "Over the coming quarters..."

All five operate below the verbatim resolution threshold.

### Base Case — NO Mention: 70–72%

Powell holds the line. He deploys the standard toolkit: geopolitical attribution (Iran war, oil spike), epistemic disclaimer (can't predict with certainty), data-dependency language (monitoring incoming data), and institutional frame shifting (the Committee's assessment). The Iran external shock framing is particularly useful — it provides a non-structural narrative for the deterioration that doesn't require recession framing.

The rate hold (consensus) removes one pressure vector. Powell isn't defending a dramatic policy action that might require recession justification. The dot plot and rate path will dominate reporter attention. Framing: "significant labor market softening" with "appropriate caution given external dynamics."

Resolution path: NO by transcript absence.

---

### Bull Case — YES Mention: 22–28%

The denial mechanism. A reporter frames the question in a way that forces Powell to use the word in his denial: *"Chair Powell, with the worst jobs report in years, are we in a recession?"*

Powell's reflex in that scenario: "I do not believe the United States is currently in a recession." That sentence resolves YES.

This path requires two things in sequence: (1) a reporter asks the direct framing, not a softer variant; (2) Powell chooses denial-with-the-word over pivot-without-the-word when cornered. Both are uncertain. The mechanism is real — it's how the 2022 testimony usage happened — but congressional hearings force it structurally. FOMC pressers don't. Powell controls his room.

**Key constraint on this path:** The -92K NFP landed two weeks ago. Reporters have cycled through the recession framing extensively. By presser day, the story may be stale — they move to dot plot and rate path questions. The Iran escalation and oil prices add alternative hooks. The direct recession question may not be the lead.

## Adversarial Notes

Five high-merit challenges survived review. These are not nitpicks — they changed the final estimate.

**1. The Goldman Inversion (High Merit)**
Goldman puts *actual* recession probability at 25%. Our pre-adversarial synthesis put Powell *mentioning* recession at 34%. Powell mention probability should be structurally lower than event probability — he avoids the word even when the event is likely. The synthesis inverted the logical relationship. Net adjustment: -3 to -5pp.

**2. The 2022 Analog Is Wrong Category (High Merit)**
The primary YES mechanism — denial-forces-the-word — was supported almost entirely by the June 2022 congressional testimony. Congressional hearings and FOMC pressers have structurally different dynamics. At pressers, Powell controls reporter selection, timing, and pivots. The actual presser base rate post-June 2022: 0 uses in approximately 15 meetings, through material data deterioration. The analog is non-comparable. Net adjustment: -3 to -4pp.

**3. Reporter Dependency Is Unmodeled (High Merit)**
The bull case hinges on a reporter asking a direct, undeflectable question. How often does this happen at FOMC pressers vs. softer variants? Which credentialed reporters will be in the room? What is Powell's historical deflection success rate at pressers when the direct question is asked? None of this was quantified. The 24% pre-adversarial bull case probability was a black box. The correct treatment is a conditional probability structure, not a point estimate.

**4. Pre-NFP Base Rate Overweighted (Medium Merit)**
The two consecutive NO resolutions (December 2025, January 2026) were used as behavioral base rate evidence. Both predate the -92K February NFP — the described structural break in the data series. Using pre-break behavior to forecast post-break behavior understates the uncertainty. Net adjustment: -1 to -2pp.

**5. Iran War Cuts Both Ways (Medium Merit)**
The synthesis correctly identified that Iran war + oil spike gives Powell an external attribution buffer for his *answers*. But the same geopolitical-economic confluence increases the *probability a reporter asks the recession question in the first place*. The synthesis conflated Powell's response framing with the question-framing incentive. These are separate variables. Net adjustment: +1 to +2pp (partially offsets other downward revisions).

## What the Market Is Missing

Very little. The market at 28¢ is more correct than the initial synthesis suggested.

**If there is any edge, it's here:**

The denial mechanism has a tail the market may be discounting. The verbatim rule means a denial *is* a YES — Powell doesn't have to affirm a recession, he just has to use the word while denying it. Reporters covering the worst jobs print in years have a direct hook. The mechanism is low-probability but real. If the market is pricing this at ~5–8% and the actual probability of the path is ~10–15%, there's minor edge on YES.

**What the market has right:**

The linguistic threshold is the binding constraint, not the macro backdrop. Traders who believe a recession is actually occurring — at whatever probability — have independently priced the *mention* market at 28¢. The separation is correct: recession happening ≠ Powell saying so tomorrow. The market understands this. The research process initially underweighted it.

**The thin market caveat:**

9,675 open interest contracts. A single position could distort the 28¢ price in either direction. This introduces noise but doesn't systematically favor our estimate over the market's — thin markets can be distorted upward or downward with equal probability.

**Open data gaps that could move the estimate:**

- Sahm Rule status: At 4.6% unemployment, likely triggered. At 4.4%, maybe not. If triggered, reporter framing tightens and YES probability rises 2–4pp.
- Q3 2025 GDP: If sub-1%, two consecutive quarters creates "consecutive" reporter framing that's harder to deflect. Unconfirmed.
- Governor Waller Feb 23 speech: If Waller avoided recession language publicly, Powell telegraphed his posture. Not read.

If both Sahm and Q3 GDP resolve unfavorably (triggered + consecutive), the range shifts to 32–36% YES and a modest YES edge re-emerges. As of market close March 17, these gaps are unresolved.
